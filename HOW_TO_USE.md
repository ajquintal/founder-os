# How to use the Founder OS

Plain English. You don't need to know how any of it works — you just talk to it.

## The idea in one line

You have a system that already knows how you think, decide, and operate. Point it at any venture and it does the heavy lifting — stress-testing ideas, researching markets, designing offers, spinning up new ventures — the way you would, without you re-explaining yourself each time.

## Using it day to day — just say what you want

You don't memorize commands. Say the thing in plain words and the right tool runs:

| You say… | What runs | You get back |
|---|---|---|
| "Stress-test this idea: …" | `/stress-test` | GO / REFINE / KILL, the make-or-break assumptions, the cheapest test |
| "Market scan for …" | `/market-scan` | Sized, sourced market + competitor brief with the wedge |
| "Design the offer / do the economics for …" | `/offer-architect` | The offer stack + the numbers, before anything's built |
| "New venture: …" (or "start …") | `/new-venture` | A ~5-minute intake, then your whole system specializes to it |

Every one of these automatically reads your Founder Profile first — your standards, voice, failure modes, and guardrails — so the output sounds like you and respects your rules (drafts only, LYV firewall, no spend without your say-so).

Those four are the everyday entry points. The full library covers the whole lifecycle — **Concept** (stress-test, market-scan, offer-architect, opportunity-size, naming-brand, go-no-go), **Launch** (positioning, direct-response-copy, landing-funnel, content-engine, paid-ads, seo-geo, launch-plan), **Operate** (metrics-dashboard, lead-response, weekly-ops-review, sop-writer, meeting-synth, content-cadence), and **Build** (venture-bootstrap, setup-checklists, product-spec, plus code-reviewer / tester / debugger subagents). Say **`/founder`** any time to see the full menu.

## Starting a new venture

Say "new venture." It asks only what it can't already figure out (a handful of questions, a few minutes), writes a one-page context, and shows you a card to approve. From that moment, every skill works on that venture too. That's the whole point — it scales to as many businesses as you run.

## How I operate it (so you don't have to babysit)

I drive. I build, research, draft, and decide the reversible things to finished, then show you the result. I only stop for four things: **spending money, signing/committing, sending anything external, or something only you can physically do.** Everything else, I just do.
