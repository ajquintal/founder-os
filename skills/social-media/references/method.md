# /social-media — Full method & worked example

One level deep; does not fan out further. Lenses: Brunson (the hook carries the post; story → offer), Dunford/positioning (every post repeats the wedge — social is positioning at volume), Gerber/E-Myth (systemize into templates + a batch so it runs without the founder), Christensen/JTBD (a post answers a buyer job or dissolves an objection), and the content-engine first principle — **capture once, atomize many, platform-native**. The loop closes on the *owned* audience, not on reach.

## First principle: concentrate, then atomize — never spray

Being on five platforms is not a strategy; it is the too-many-fronts failure mode wearing an "omnichannel" costume. A founder weak on social does not need more surfaces — he needs **one anchor** where the buyer already is and his asset base is warmest, plus at most **one amplifier** that gets fed by the anchor's fan-out (zero net-new). Every other platform goes on an explicit **skip** list with a reason. The plan's discipline is visible in what it refuses to do.

This skill does **not** re-derive pillars or a recurring calendar — those are `/content-engine` and `/content-cadence`. It supplies the platform-specific layer those two point at but don't fill: which platforms, the per-platform playbook, the native templates and hooks, the platform-native derivatives, the engagement routine, and the social KPIs.

## 1. Platform selection — the fit matrix

Score each candidate on: **ICP fit** (is the buyer's *purchasing* attention here, not just eyeballs), **native form** (can the venture produce this on its constraints — time, video capacity), **buyer intent**, **effort**, and the **venture's warmest asset** (existing audience/handle). Then pick 1 anchor + ≤1 amplifier.

| Platform | ICP fit (who buys here) | Native form | Buyer intent | Effort | Pick when · Skip when |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **LinkedIn** | B2B execs, operators, founders, budget-holders | text posts, doc-carousels, long-form | High (professional identity + authority to spend) | Low–med | **Pick:** B2B, considered/high-ACV, founder-led. Tony's warmest owned audience → **default B2B anchor**. **Skip:** pure impulse B2C. |
| **X / Twitter** | founders, tech, finance, operators, media | threads, standalone posts, replies | Med (discovery + authority; weak direct buy) | Med | **Pick:** thought-leadership, tech/finance ICP, as a B2B **amplifier** of the LinkedIn anchor. **Skip:** local/visual-first consumer. |
| **Instagram** | consumers 25–45, lifestyle, local, aspirational | Reels, carousels, Stories | Med (aspirational + local discovery) | Med–high | **Pick:** visual/experiential B2C (hospitality, product, design, food). **Skip:** dry B2B SaaS. |
| **YouTube** | high-intent researchers, all ages, long attention | long-form video, Shorts | High (search intent + deep trust) | High | **Pick:** complex/considered purchase, education-led, evergreen SEO. **Skip:** no video capacity, or a speed-to-market sprint. |
| **TikTok** | 16–35 consumers, broad trend-driven reach | short vertical video | Low–med (discovery; weak direct buy) | High | **Pick:** broad consumer reach, brand-building, younger ICP. **Skip:** B2B, premium/exclusive, thin team. |
| **Others** (Threads, Facebook, Pinterest, Reddit, YouTube Shorts) | context-dependent | — | — | — | Niche — only if the ICP is *provably* concentrated there. Default: skip. |

**Selection rule.** One anchor = where buyer intent × warm asset is highest. One optional amplifier = the cheapest place to re-run the anchor's fan-out. Everything else skips until the anchor is producing outcomes. Adding a platform is adding a front — it must beat the anchor on ICP fit or it is a distraction with a content calendar.

## 2. Per-platform playbook (one card per chosen platform)

- **Role** — anchor (net-new + primary) or amplifier (derivatives only).
- **Objective** — the outcome it exists for (booked calls / list adds / discovery), not "presence".
- **Formats (ranked)** — the platform-native forms, best first.
- **Frequency** — sized to what a cheap model can draft and Tony can approve in *one* batch review. Sustainable beats ambitious.
- **What it rewards** — the algorithmic truth (dwell, early comments, saves, watch-time, replies) — exploited honestly, never gamed with bait.
- **The one thing** — the single lever that decides the platform (LinkedIn: the first line; X: the hook tweet; IG/short-form: the 0–3s hook).

## 4. Template pack — paste-ready, `[BRACKETS]` to fill

The heart of the deliverable. These carry the founder: he approves and queues, he does not write from a blank page. All voice-checked — clinical, precise, premium; no banned words; no hype register.

### LinkedIn hook library (the first line is the *only* line shown before "…more" — it earns the click)
- **Named enemy:** "[Common practice] is a receipt, not a result."
- **Contrarian:** "Everyone in [category] optimizes [X]. It's the wrong lever."
- **Result/number:** "[Specific outcome] in [timeframe]. Here's the exact [system]."
- **Confession/transformation:** "[Timeframe] ago, [before-state]. Today, [after-state]. What changed wasn't [the obvious guess]."
- **The expensive mistake:** "The costliest mistake [ICP] make with [thing]: [mistake]."
- **The indicting question:** "You optimized your [X]. Why does your [Y] still run on guesswork?"
- **Status reframe:** "The gap between [amateur] and [operator] isn't [assumed]. It's [real]."
- **List promise:** "[N] [things] that [outcome]. The one nobody does is #[k]."

### LinkedIn Template A — POV / transformation (Tony's proven format; lead with it)
```
[HOOK — one line. Contrarian / result / confession. The ONLY line before "…more". Earn the click.]

[1–2 short lines: the before-state or the context. One idea per line, white space between.]

[The turn: what changed / the insight / the mechanism — the wedge, in plain language.]

[The proof or concrete detail — a number, a case, the specific move. No medical/legal claims.]

[The lesson generalized to the reader — the pillar's claim.]

[Soft CTA — a question that invites comments, or a low-friction next step. No hard sell.]
```
*(Put any external link in the first comment, not the post body — LinkedIn suppresses in-post links.)*

### LinkedIn Template B — teardown / framework (text, or a doc-carousel via Canva)
```
[HOOK — the promise of a framework or teardown.]

Most [ICP] do [X]. Here's the [N]-step system that actually [outcome]:

1. [Step — verb first] — [one line why]
2. [Step] — [why]
3. [Step] — [why]

The part everyone skips: [the wedge / the non-obvious step].

[Soft CTA — where to go deeper.]
```

### LinkedIn Template C — proof / case (anonymized; guardrail-safe)
```
[HOOK — the result, anonymized.]

[The situation — the before, the constraint. Anonymized; no claim the venture can't deliver.]

What changed: [the mechanism, mapped to the pillar].

The result: [specific, honest outcome — no medical/regulatory claim; no live-dashboard implication].

Why it worked: [the wedge].

[Soft CTA.]
```

## 6. Anchor → platform-native fan-out

One anchor asset (from content-engine's pipeline) → its platform-native derivatives. Each is *reformatted*, not copy-pasted. Nothing net-new.
```
1 ANCHOR (LinkedIn long-form POV essay)
├─ X thread                    → the argument, atomized (7 tweets)
├─ LinkedIn doc-carousel       → the framework as 5 slides (Canva MCP)
├─ 2–3 X standalone posts       → the hook line / the mechanism / the proof
├─ 2 quote-cards               → strongest lines as image posts (Canva MCP)
└─ 1 owned-list email          → the same argument → CTA to the mapped offer rung (Gmail draft)
```
If a "derivative" needs fresh research, it is a new anchor — schedule it, don't smuggle it in.

## 7. Engagement / community routine (the part founders skip)

Time-boxed, delegatable to a cheap model that drafts → Tony approves in the batch. Drafts-only on anything outbound.

- **Daily (≤15 min):** (1) reply to every comment on your own last post first — fastest algo signal + relationship; (2) leave a substantive comment on 5–10 ICP / target-account posts (deposit before you withdraw — a real point, never "great post"); (3) draft replies to DMs (**never auto-send** — guardrail).
- **Weekly (≤30 min, batched):** touch the 20–30 "dream accounts" (ICP decision-makers + referrers, **non-LYV**) with a thoughtful comment or share; harvest the sharpest questions/objections from your comments into the content backlog (comments are free market research → next anchors).
- **Rules:** brand voice always; never argue; DMs drafts-only; ToS-safe (no automation, no pods); the whole routine is a batch a cheaper model prepares and Tony approves.

## 8. Growth loops + the social KPI set

### Growth loops (compounding — not one-shot posting)
1. **Content → owned-audience loop (the one that matters):** post → profile visit → follow/connect → captured to the **owned list** (email/DM opt-in) → nurtured → offer. Social's job is to feed the owned asset; the loop closes on a list add, not a like.
2. **Engagement flywheel:** comment on ICP posts → they visit your profile → follow → your posts reach them → they engage → repeat. Manual, compounding, ToS-safe.
3. **Repurpose / recycle:** a winner → the evergreen bank → re-run on rotation (handed to `/content-cadence`). Winners compound; you don't reinvent.
4. **Borrowed-audience loop:** collaborations, guest threads, being quoted/tagged by larger ICP accounts → access to their audience. One warm collaboration beats a month of cold posting.

### Social KPI set → `/metrics-dashboard`
Leading-weighted; every metric carries a floor → action; vanity counts are demoted to diagnostics. Hand this block to `/metrics-dashboard` to sit under the venture north-star.

| Metric | L/L | Definition | Source | Cadence | Floor → Action |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ICP replies/comments on own posts | leading | substantive comments from *target-ICP* profiles (not "nice post") | native analytics + manual tag | weekly | `<[floor]`/post for 2 wks → change the hook or pillar |
| Profile visits from target ICP | leading | profile views — reach landing on the right people | native analytics | weekly | flat 2 wks → hook not landing; revise |
| DM conversations started | leading | qualified inbound/opened DM threads | manual | weekly | 0 for 2 wks → add a CTA that invites DMs |
| Saves + shares | leading | intent signals (save = "I'll act"; share = advocacy) | native | weekly | `<[floor]` → content not useful enough |
| Owned-list adds from social | leading→money | new email/DM opt-ins attributed to social | UTM + form | weekly | `<[floor]` → tighten the profile CTA / lead magnet |
| Primary conversions attributed to social | lagging (money) | the venture's core conversion where source = social — booked calls/demos (B2B), purchases/checkouts (e-comm/B2C), trials/signups (self-serve), bookings (local/hospitality) | CRM / store / booking tool + UTM | weekly | 0 by `[gate date]` → the anchor is cut/reallocated (kill discipline) |
| Revenue attributed to social | lagging (money) | closed $ traced to social-sourced leads/orders | CRM / store | monthly | — (confirms the loop) |

**Demoted to diagnostics — never the scorecard:** follower count, impressions, likes. (Matches `/metrics-dashboard`: no vanity counts.) **North-star candidate for social:** owned-list adds *or* the venture's primary conversion attributed to social (booked calls / purchases / signups / bookings) — never follower count.

## Worked example (abridged) — Executive Edge OS, 90-day B2B sprint

**1. Platform selection (fit matrix applied).**
- **Anchor = LinkedIn.** Execs + operators buy here; buyer intent is highest for cohorts/licenses; and it's Tony's warmest asset (~3,217 warm connections; the Nov 2025 transformation post did strong numbers). Highest intent × warmest asset → anchor.
- **Amplifier = X.** Operators, founders, finance/tech; cheap to re-run the LinkedIn thread. Derivatives only, zero net-new.
- **Skip: Instagram** (visual-first B2C-lifestyle mismatch, wrong buyer intent), **TikTok** (younger, low B2B intent), **YouTube** (high production cost, too slow for a 90-day sprint). Reason: a 90-day sprint at ≤2 hrs/week with no video capacity → concentrate where the buyer and the warm asset already are.

**2. Per-platform playbook.**
- *LinkedIn (anchor).* Objective: booked B2B calls (cohorts/licenses) via authority + warm-audience activation. Formats ranked: (1) POV/transformation text post (Tony's proven format), (2) teardown/framework (text or doc-carousel), (3) proof/case. Frequency: 3/wk (Mon/Wed/Fri), one batch approval. Rewards: dwell + early comments (reply fast) + saves; suppresses in-post links → link in first comment. **The one thing:** the first line.
- *X (amplifier).* Objective: extend reach to operators/founders + repurpose. Formats: the LinkedIn essay → thread + 2–3 standalones. Frequency: 3–4/wk, all derived. Rewards: replies + reposts + bookmarks. **The one thing:** the hook tweet stands alone.

## Anti-patterns (flag these)

- Being on five platforms "to be everywhere" (too-many-fronts). → 1 anchor + ≤1 amplifier + an explicit skip list.
- Cross-posting identical text everywhere (not platform-native). → reformat per platform.
- Scorecarding followers / chasing a viral format off-thesis (vanity + drift). → owned-list adds + booked calls; stay on the wedge.
- Net-new content per platform (founder-dependence + overbuild). → one anchor, atomized.
- Perfecting one post for days (standards-as-bottleneck). → ship the batch, iterate on comments.
- Hype/emoji-bait register to "beat the algorithm" (voice breach). → clinical; repeat the wedge; earn attention with the first line.
- Auto-DM tools, bought followers, engagement pods (ToS + reputation risk). → manual, ToS-safe, drafts-only.
