# Lead Response — Method

## Principle
Every inbound is triaged before it's answered. The draft is a finished asset — paste-ready — but the send is always the founder's. Route to the offer that fits, not the one that prices highest; a mis-sold A-tier churns and costs more than it books.

## Step 1 — Read the lead
Capture: identity/role, company (for B2B), the explicit ask, channel (email / form / DM / LinkedIn), and any signal (deadline, budget mention, "how much", competitor named, volume).

## Step 2 — Qualify (triage rubric)
Score three axes, High/Med/Low, one line each:
- **Fit** — do they match the ICP in venture-context? (role, segment, problem).
- **Intent** — buying-signal strength: urgency, specificity, budget/authority cues.
- **Value** — $ potential and which offer/tier they map to.

Roll up:
- **Tier A** — high Fit + medium/high Intent, meaningful Value → live conversation.
- **Tier B** — fits but early, or fits a lower tier → nurture / entry offer.
- **Tier C** — poor fit, or fully served by self-serve → self-serve link or polite decline.

## Step 3 — Route
Map the tier to the venture's **actual conversion motion** — a SaaS demo/scoping call is one case, not the default. Read the offer set from venture-context and pick the real next step:

| Tier | Motion | CTA (by archetype — use the venture's actual next step) |
|---|---|---|
| A | The offer's real high-intent step | high-touch/B2B/services → scoping/sales call (booking link); goods/DTC → direct purchase/checkout link; marketplace → platform signup or a booking; wholesale → line-sheet + application |
| B | Nurture / entry offer | entry-tier offer or sequence enrollment |
| C | Self-serve / decline | self-serve link or courteous no |

Route to the highest-**fit** offer from the ladder in venture-context. Don't default an A-tier goods buyer to "book a call" or a marketplace lead to a sales demo — route to the step that actually converts that offer.

## Step 4 — Flag risk
- **Claims review** — the lead invites a regulated claim (health / financial / legal / environmental) or any outcome promise → do not assert it; draft around it and flag for professional review, honoring the venture's own claims & regulatory constraints.
- **LYV firewall** — any crossover with LYV → do not route or reference; flag.
- **Approval** — discount, custom pricing, or spend implied → mark NEEDS approval, don't commit.

## Step 5 — Draft the reply
Founder voice: clinical, precise, direct, active. Structure: (1) acknowledge their stated problem in one line, (2) name the fitting offer and the single relevant proof point, (3) one CTA. Match channel length (email = short paragraphs; DM = 3–5 lines). No banned words (journey, holistic, revolutionary, game-changer, guru, hack). A/B variant only when the CTA itself differs.

## Step 6 — Recommend next action
State the one move: "Send draft A, then on reply send booking link X." Recommendation only — nothing is executed.

## Worked examples — Executive Edge OS
Ladder: freemium → $199 → ~$99/mo → $4,997; B2B cohorts $30–48k, licenses $25k + $2k.

### Example 1 — B2B, Tier A
**Inbound (email):** "I'm Chief of Staff at a PE firm. Several partners are burning out; do you run executive health programs for teams? What does it cost?"
**Triage:** Fit High (B2B exec cohort ICP) · Intent High (team need + price question) · Value High ($30–48k cohort) → **Tier A**.
**Route:** founder cohort scoping call. Flag: no medical claims.
**Draft (email):**
> Subject: Executive health for your partners — cohort scoping
>
> [Name] — burnout across a partner group is a measurable, fixable problem, and we run it as a protocol rather than a perk. Executive Edge OS fuses blood panels with wearable data into an execution layer each partner follows weekly, with a quarterly cohort-level report. We deliver this as a fixed cohort engagement. Worth a 20-minute scoping call to size it to your partner count and target outcomes — here's my calendar: [booking link]. — Tony

**Next action:** Send draft, then on reply share the cohort scoping calendar. Flags: claims-review (outcomes kept non-medical); no pricing quoted pending scope.

### Example 2 — B2C, claims risk, Tier B
**Inbound (DM):** "Will this cure my thyroid issue and get me off my meds?"
**Triage:** Fit Med (individual, health-motivated) · Intent Med · Value Low → entry ($199) → **Tier B**.
**Route:** $199 entry offer. Flag: **claims-review** — cannot promise a cure or medication change.
**Draft (DM):**
> Appreciate you reaching out. I can't speak to your medications or diagnose anything — that stays between you and your physician. What Executive Edge OS does is build a precise, data-driven protocol from your bloodwork and wearable data, so you and your doctor can see what's actually moving. The $199 baseline is the place to start: [link]. — Tony

**Next action:** Send draft. Flag: claims-review before send; no medical / med-change language.
