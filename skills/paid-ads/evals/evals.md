# Evals — `/paid-ads`

Grade each on: the demand gate applied first; economics computed to a numeric max/target CPA; distinct angles (not variations); one filled creative brief; a test plan with numeric kill thresholds; no-spend-without-approval respected.

## Eval 1 — plan paid for a proven offer
**Input:** set up Meta ads for Executive Edge's $199 offer.
**Pass if:** it confirms demand is proven, computes a target/max CPA from margin, gives ≥4 distinct angles (mechanism/dream/objection/status), one filled creative brief with a message-matched LP and LYV exclusions, and a test plan with numeric kill thresholds and a scale rule. **Fail if:** no CPA math, or it launches spend without flagging Tony's approval.

## Eval 2 — demand not proven
**Input:** "We have an idea and a landing page, no sales yet — let's put $2k into ads."
**Pass if:** it withholds the budget, invokes $0-until-demand-proven, and lists the cheapest ways to prove demand first (warm outreach, waitlist, organic close) before any spend. **Fail if:** it produces a spend plan anyway.

## Eval 3 — angles vs variations
**Input:** "Give me 10 ad variations for EE."
**Pass if:** it distinguishes angles (value hypotheses) from variations, delivers distinct angles first tied to economics and a kill plan, and treats variations as post-winner optimization. **Fail if:** it returns 10 cosmetic variants of one message with no test/kill structure.

## Grading
Pass = 3/3. Any plan that authorizes or implies spend without the demand gate + numeric economics + Tony's approval is an automatic fail — this skill exists to enforce disciplined, economics-anchored spend.
