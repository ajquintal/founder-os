# /paid-ads — Full method & worked example

One level deep; does not fan out further. Lenses: Hormozi (angles from core desires + objections), Christensen/JTBD (an angle is a job the buyer hires the product for), Scale Mechanics (spend only against proven front-end economics), Skok (CAC, payback, ROAS).

## The gate before the plan

Paid media does not create demand; it pours fuel on it. If the offer doesn't convert warm/organic traffic, paid buys expensive proof of the same failure. **$0 until demand is proven** (organic sales, a converting waitlist, or a warm close rate). If that proof doesn't exist, the deliverable is not an ad plan — it's "here's the cheapest way to prove demand first," and paid waits.

## Economics = the discipline anchor

Pull from `/offer-architect`:
- **Margin** per front-end sale → **break-even ROAS = 1 ÷ margin**.
- **Max CPA** = contribution margin on the front-end sale (spend more than this and the first order loses money).
- **Target CPA** = the CPA that hits the payback window with room to profit (e.g., ½ of max).
- If proven backend (recurring/ascension) shortens payback, the *ceiling* can rise later — but tests start against front-end economics, not hoped-for LTV.

Every angle, budget, and kill decision references these numbers. No number → no spend.

## Angles ≠ variations

An **angle** is a distinct value hypothesis — a different reason the buyer acts. A **variation** is the same angle in a new color/thumbnail. Test angles first (they move results), variations second (they optimize a proven winner). Angle sources:

| Angle type | The lever (Hormozi / JTBD) |
|---|---|
| Mechanism | *how* it works — the unique method/wedge |
| Dream outcome | the result in the buyer's words |
| Objection-crusher | dissolve the #1 reason they don't buy |
| Status / identity | who they become |
| Speed / simplicity | less time, less effort (value-equation denominator) |

## Hooks

2–3 per angle. First line is a pattern interrupt. Structure: **problem** (name the pain), **promise** (the shift), **proof** (why believe). Match the hook to the landing page — message mismatch kills conversion faster than any bid.

## Creative brief (fill once, reuse)

Angle · Hook (lead line) · Format (e.g., 30–45s vertical video, static, carousel) · Primary text · Headline · CTA · Audience/targeting (cold interest, lookalike %, exclusions) · Landing page (message-matched) · Success metric (target CPA + ceiling).

## Test plan + kill thresholds

Structure it as gates, not a spray:

| Stage | Variable | Budget | Spend cap | Kill | Scale |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| T1 — angle test | N angles, 1 ad each, same audience | small/ad | ~1× max CPA per angle | angle spends 1× max CPA with 0 sales, or CTR below floor at N impressions | winner ≤ target CPA → T2 |
| T2 — audience test | winning angle × few audiences | small/audience | ~2× target CPA per audience | same CTR floor; audience over ceiling | ROAS ≥ break-even × 1.3 → T3 |
| T3 — scale | duplicate winners, raise budget | +20% / 3 days | CPA over ceiling 3 days → pull back | — | hold CPA while raising budget |

Discipline: the spend cap is a hard stop. A losing angle dies at the cap. No "give it one more day" — that's how a $500 test becomes a $5k lesson.

## Worked example (abridged) — Executive Edge OS, $199 front-end

**Demand gate:** EE has freemium labs uploads + organic $199 sales → demand proven → small paid tests warranted (within the $10k ceiling, on approval).

**Economics (illustrative — replace with live `/offer-architect` numbers):** front offer $199, ~85% margin → contribution ≈ $169. Break-even ROAS ≈ 1.18. **Max CPA ≈ $169** (front-end break-even). **Target CPA ≈ $85** (2:1 on contribution). Proven $199→$99/mo conversion can lift the ceiling later; tests start at front-end math.

**Angles:**
| # | Angle | Lens | Desire/objection | Hook | Rung |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Blood + device fusion → a protocol, not a PDF | Mechanism | certainty / "another dashboard" | "Your wearable and your bloodwork have never spoken to each other." | $199 |
| 2 | Know exactly what moves your numbers, weekly | Dream outcome | control | "You optimized your calendar. Your body still runs on guesswork." | $199 |
| 3 | You don't need another lab — you need to execute the one you have | Objection | already spent on testing | "A $400 blood panel you didn't act on is a receipt, not a result." | $199 |
| 4 | Run your body like a P&L | Status/identity | identity | "The operating system high-performers use to run their body like a P&L." | $199→$99/mo |

**Creative brief (angle 1):** Hook above · 30–45s vertical UGC/VO · primary text: clinical, 3–4 lines on the fusion + closed loop · headline "From data to protocol." · CTA "Run your first protocol — $199." · targeting: men 30–55, wearable/optimization interests, high-income geos, lookalike 1–3% of freemium signups, **exclude existing customers + all LYV audiences** · LP: /protocol message-matched · success: CPA ≤ $85, ceiling $169.

**Test plan:** T1 — 4 angles × $10/day, kill any at $169 spend with 0 sales or CTR <1% at 2k impressions; winner ≤ $85 CPA → T2 across 2–3 audiences; T3 scale +20%/3 days while CPA holds under ceiling.

**Single metric (lives/dies):** front-end CPA vs max CPA — hold CPA at/under the $85 target and never above the $169 break-even ceiling; if it can't hold under the ceiling, the plan dies.

**Guardrails applied:** no spend until Tony approves; only substantiated claims, regulated claims routed to review (per the venture's claims guardrails); drafts only; LYV audiences excluded.

## Anti-patterns (flag these)

- Running ads before organic converts (paying to prove a broken offer).
- "Scale it, it'll work at volume" on an angle that failed its cap (throwing good money after bad).
- 10 variations of one angle presented as a "test" (no distinct hypotheses).
- Bidding against hoped-for LTV before the backend conversion is proven.
