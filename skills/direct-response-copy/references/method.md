# /direct-response-copy — Full method & worked example

One level deep; does not fan out further. Lenses: Hormozi (value equation, offer stack, unique mechanism), Brunson (hook → story → offer; test multiple hooks).

## The skeleton (why this order)

**Hook → Problem → Mechanism → Offer → CTA.** Attention first, then desire, then belief, then the ask. Most weak copy skips the **mechanism** — it promises an outcome without explaining why *this* works when the reader's past attempts didn't. The mechanism is where positioning's wedge does its job.

- **Hook.** Earn the next line. Lead on the dream outcome in the buyer's own words — a promise, a curiosity gap, or a pattern-interrupt. Brunson: the hook is most of the battle, so draft several and test. Deliver the lead hook + 2 alternates every time.
- **Problem.** Name the struggling moment (the job the buyer is stuck on) and agitate the *cost of the status quo*. Tony's voice bans fear-mongering — make the cost real and specific, not catastrophized.
- **Mechanism.** The unique mechanism: the differentiated *how* that makes the promise believable. This is the belief shift — "here's why this works when [the alternative] didn't." Ties straight to the positioning wedge.
- **Offer.** Stack the value against the value equation — raise dream outcome × likelihood, lower time delay × effort. State price/terms and a risk reversal if one exists. Pull the actual offer from `/offer-architect`; never invent price.
- **CTA.** One action, stated plainly. Real scarcity/urgency only (a genuine cap or deadline) — never a manufactured countdown.

## Voice pass (before the draft ships)

Enforce `voice-and-brand.md`: power vocabulary (protocol, optimize, system, calibrate, compound, leverage); active voice; lead with the point; ≤2 emphasis moves per passage. Strip banned words (journey, wellness-as-vibe, holistic, hack, revolutionary, game-changer, guru) and any fake urgency. Then attach the DRAFT line.
