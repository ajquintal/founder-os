# Evals — `/direct-response-copy`

Grade each on: the five beats present and in order (hook → problem → mechanism → offer → CTA), 2 alternate hooks, Tony's voice enforced (power words in, banned words + fake urgency out), the DRAFT line present, and no claim the venture can't deliver.

## Eval 1 — sales-page section (calibration)
**Input:** write the $199-unlock hero section for Executive Edge, warm audience.
**Pass if:** hook leads on the dream outcome in the buyer's words; the mechanism beat names the closed loop / blood-device fusion; offer states $199 (pulled, not invented) with a single CTA; 2 alt hooks; DRAFT line; no "physician review" / medical claim, no banned words, no fake urgency. **Fail if:** the mechanism beat is missing, price is invented, or a banned word / fake countdown appears.

## Eval 2 — email (industry-agnostic)
**Input:** write a warm-audience email for a generic B2B SaaS driving to a booked demo.
**Pass if:** HOOK renders as a subject line; the five beats hold in the body; one CTA (book demo); 2 alt subject-line hooks; DRAFT line; voice enforced. **Fail if:** multiple competing CTAs, no subject line, or hype / fake urgency creeps in.

## Eval 3 — ad (short-form, cold)
**Input:** write a cold-traffic ad (headline + primary text) for any venture's entry offer.
**Pass if:** HOOK = a scroll-stopping headline; the beats compress but the mechanism still appears; one CTA; 2 alt headlines; DRAFT line; respects claims limits. **Fail if:** it's a hype blurb with no mechanism, or makes an unverifiable claim.

## Grading
Pass = 3/3, each with all five beats, 2 alt hooks, the DRAFT line, and a clean voice pass. Any banned word, fake urgency, invented price, or un-deliverable claim fails that case outright.
