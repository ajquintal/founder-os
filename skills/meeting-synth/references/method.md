# /meeting-synth — Full method & worked example

One level deep; does not fan out further. Lens: Harnish (Scaling Up) — a meeting rhythm only compounds if it ends in owned actions and closed decisions, not notes.

## The discipline

Raw notes blur three different things: what was *decided*, what someone *will do*, and what's *still open*. The synthesis separates them cleanly, and refuses to manufacture the parts the meeting didn't actually produce — owners, dates, commitments. The summary is written last, in Tony's voice, and leads with the outcome.

## The six moves

1. **Ingest.** Capture meeting, date, attendees if present. Read the whole thing before extracting — decisions often reverse earlier discussion.
2. **Decisions.** A decision reached *closure* — a choice made, a direction set. "We talked about X" is not a decision; "we're doing X" is. Attach an owner if one was named.
3. **Action items.** A concrete next action with an *owner* and a *due date*. If the source doesn't state an owner or date, mark it `—` (unassigned / no-date) — never infer one. A missing owner on an important action becomes an open question.
4. **Open questions.** Everything unresolved: debated-but-not-decided, blockers, parked items. Phrase each as a question and name who can answer it.
5. **Summary.** One paragraph in Tony's register — direct, active, no agenda recap. Lead with what moved and what happens next, not "the team met to discuss."
6. **Guardrail pass.** Strip fabricated commitments; flag (don't send) any external follow-up; no regulated claims (health / financial / legal / environmental). Follow-up messages are drafts.

## Worked example (abridged) — B2B sprint sync (Executive Edge)

**Raw notes (in):** "B2B sprint sync 7/10, Tony + Claude. Pipeline: 2 warm corp contacts, 0 booked calls. Decided: productize the cohort at a fixed $30k/6-mo floor, drop custom quoting for now. Claude to draft outreach to the 2 contacts by 7/12 (drafts only). Unsure whether to lead with cohort or operator license for the med-group contact. Aug 3 kill date stands. Tony must approve copy before anything goes out."

**Synthesis (out):**
```
SUMMARY:
  The sprint holds 2 warm corporate contacts and zero booked calls, so the priority is converting
  warm demand fast. The cohort is now a fixed $30k/6-mo offer — custom quoting is dropped to shorten
  the cycle — and outreach drafts go to Tony this week. The Aug 3 kill date on dead rails stands.
DECISIONS:
  - Productize the cohort at a fixed $30k/6-mo floor              (owner: Tony)
  - Drop custom quoting for now                                  (owner: Tony)
ACTION ITEMS:
  - [ ] Draft outreach to the 2 warm corporate contacts (drafts only) — owner: Claude — due: 7/12
  - [ ] Approve outreach copy before anything sends — owner: Tony — due: —
OPEN QUESTIONS:
  - Lead with the cohort or the operator license for the med-group contact?   (who can answer: Tony)
```

Note what it refused to do: no invented due date on Tony's approval; the unresolved lead-with question stayed an open question rather than being promoted to a decision; and the outreach is a draft pending approval — drafts-only, honored structurally, not as a caveat.
