# /naming-brand — Full method & worked example

One level deep; does not fan out further. Lens: April Dunford (positioning) + the Founder Profile voice engine.

## Why criteria before candidates

A name shortlist ranked on gut feel is not a finished asset. Fix the rubric first, then generate against it — that makes the screen defensible and stops the best-sounding name from beating the best-positioned one.

## The weighted rubric (tune weights per venture)

| Criterion | What it tests | Default weight |
|---|---|---|
| Distinctiveness / ownability | Not generic; clears the category's existing names | 20 |
| Positioning fit | Signals or is compatible with the wedge; won't mislead the ICP | 20 |
| Voice fit | Premium/clinical register; **zero banned words** | 15 |
| Pronounce / spell | Says cleanly, spells from hearing, survives a phone call | 10 |
| Extensibility | Stretches across the value ladder + future lines; not locked to one product/price | 15 |
| Domain / handle plausibility | A usable exact or near domain + social handles likely obtainable | 10 |
| Trademark-collision risk | No obvious same-class conflict (flag only) | 10 |

Score each candidate 1–5 per criterion, weight, sum to a /100 score.

## Generate across archetypes

Force spread so the batch isn't one flavor:
- **Descriptive** — says what it does (clear, low distinctiveness).
- **Evocative / metaphor** — points at the outcome or feeling.
- **Coined / invented** — most ownable, needs meaning built.
- **Compound** — two real morphemes fused.
- **Lexical** — a real word repurposed into the category.
- **Founder / heritage** — person, place, or origin.

Aim for 12–18 candidates, ≥2 per archetype you use.

## Domain & trademark: flag, do not clear

State a risk level (likely-open / contested / taken-looking) from what's observable, and name the obvious collisions. Never assert a name is legally clear — that requires a formal search. Every shortlist ends with: run a formal trademark search in-class + confirm the live domain/handles **before commit**; purchase and filing happen only on Tony's approval (spend + commitment).

## Brand-direction brief (top pick + runner-up)

- **Positioning statement** — for [ICP] who [job], [name] is the [category] that [wedge]; unlike [incumbent], it [differentiator]. (Dunford form.)
- **Voice register** — the Founder Profile default plus any venture delta; two do's, two don'ts.
- **Verbal identity** — tagline *direction* (not a locked line), and the sub-brand/product naming pattern so the family stays coherent.
- **Avoid-list** — words, tones, and lookalike names to stay clear of.
- **Before you commit** — formal TM search · domain + handle purchase on approval · visual identity deferred to the brand kit · drafts only.

## Worked example (abridged)

**Input:** name the EE **B2B corporate cohort** offer as a sub-brand (context: Executive Edge; wedge = closed-loop execution + blood/device fusion; voice = clinical/premium).

**Criteria:** as above; extensibility and voice fit weighted up (it must sit under "Executive Edge" and stretch to future team products).

**Auto-disqualified:** any candidate leaning on "wellness journey" / "holistic" / "transformation guru" register.

**Brand-direction brief — "Executive Edge Boardroom":**
- **Positioning:** For leadership teams who refuse performance decline, Executive Edge Boardroom is the clinical performance program that runs each executive on a closed protocol loop and reports the team's baseline movement — unlike coaching or lab panels, it is the execution layer, not advice.
- **Voice:** clinical, precise, premium. Do: lead with the measured outcome, name the loop. Don't: promise a capability the venture can't deliver yet, or make a regulated claim without routing it to review (honor the venture's claims & regulatory constraints).
- **Verbal identity:** tagline direction — *"The performance operating system for your leadership team."* Sub-brand pattern: `Executive Edge <Noun>` (Boardroom, Operator, Private).
- **Before you commit:** formal TM search on "Boardroom" in-class; confirm handles; purchase on approval; drafts only.
