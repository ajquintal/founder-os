# Evals — `/naming-brand`

Grade each on: criteria fixed *before* candidates, a ranked table (≥10 rows, archetype spread), domain/TM notes that flag without asserting clearance, and a brand-direction brief consistent with the venture's wedge + the Founder Profile voice.

## Eval 1 — venture with context (calibration)
**Context:** Executive Edge (`ventures/executive-edge`).
**Input:** "Name the new single-executive concierge tier as a sub-brand" — a distinct offer from the worked example's B2B cohort.
**Pass if:** the rubric weights extensibility + voice fit; candidates span ≥3 archetypes; names sit coherently under "Executive Edge" without colliding with the cohort sub-brand; the top pick's brief states the wedge (closed-loop + blood/device) and respects EE's language guardrail (no "medical team"/"physician review", no pre-contract team-dashboard promise); every candidate carries a flag-only domain/TM note.
**Fail if:** it asserts a name is "available"/"clear", ignores the voice register, reuses the worked-example table verbatim, or invents a name that boxes EE into one price rung.

## Eval 2 — net-new venture, no context (industry-agnostic proof)
**Input:** "Name a premium B2B service that installs revenue systems for mid-market companies — no venture context yet."
**Pass if:** it works from the idea + Founder Profile voice, sets criteria first, generates across archetypes, and lands a positioning-consistent brief — without importing any health/EE conventions.
**Fail if:** it hard-codes a category's naming tropes, skips the criteria step, or produces names with no screening.

## Eval 3 — the disqualification + collision catch
**Input:** "I like 'WellnessJourney Pro' and 'Stripe Health' — score them and tell me if they're good."
**Pass if:** "WellnessJourney Pro" is **auto-disqualified** on voice (banned register: journey + wellness-as-vibe); "Stripe Health" is flagged as a high trademark-collision risk against an established mark and sent to formal search — neither is endorsed, and better on-voice alternatives are offered.
**Fail if:** it scores the banned-word name well, or declares either name "clear"/"safe to use."

## Grading
Pass = 3/3 with required elements. A run that asserts legal clearance (instead of flagging) or scores a banned-register name highly is a critical failure — those two rules are non-negotiable for this skill.
