# /market-scan — Full method & worked example

One level deep; does not fan out further.

## The fan-out (why four blind angles)

Running size / pricing / positioning / gaps as *separate* searches stops one framing from contaminating the others. Each angle is blind to the rest; you reconcile at the end. This surfaces gaps a single "tell me about the X market" query buries.

- **Size & momentum:** TAM, CAGR, recent funding rounds, category growth. Prefer analyst figures with a year attached.
- **Incumbents & pricing:** pull real prices from pricing pages / plans / filings. Capture the model (one-time / subscription / usage), not just the number.
- **Positioning:** how each player describes itself in its own words (hero copy, category label). Reveals where the category is crowded.
- **Gaps & complaints:** reviews, forums, Reddit, churned-user threads. What buyers say is missing is the raw material for the wedge.

## Reconcile

Drop anything you can't source. Where sources disagree, present the range and note the disagreement. Convert every retained claim into "fact (source)".

## Positioning table

| Competitor | Price / model | What they do | Weakness we exploit |
|---|---|---|---|

Aim for the real leaders plus the closest analog to this venture. The right-hand column is the point of the whole exercise.

## The wedge

State, in one sentence, the single differentiated thing none of the incumbents ship that this venture can own — grounded in a gap the research actually found, not a hope. Then name the riskiest unconfirmed assumption behind it.

## Worked example (abridged)

**Input:** market-scan for a "clinical daily-execution layer" (context: Executive Edge).

- **Market read:** wellness-tech ≈ $65B (2026) → ≈ $208B (2035) [source]; personalized-health AI heavily funded [source]. Momentum: high.
- **Table:** Whoop ($30/mo, passive tracking, *no protocol/coaching*); Function ($365/yr, lab panels, *no daily execution*); Marek ($250/mo, TRT focus, *no daily scoring loop*); Noom ($70/mo, generic, *not clinical / not this buyer*).
- **Pricing benchmark:** device $30/mo ↔ labs $199–365/yr ↔ concierge $250/mo. A daily-execution + labs bundle can anchor above device-only and justify a premium tier.
- **Wedge:** the closed-loop execution layer (protocol → score → drift → adjust) fusing blood + continuous-device data — none of the above closes that loop. **Riskiest unconfirmed:** whether buyers will pay a premium for "execution" vs. cheaper "insights."
