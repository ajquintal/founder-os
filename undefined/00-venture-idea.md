# Emberline — Venture Idea (pressure-test)

> **Why this venture exists (for the OS):** to prove the Founder OS works on a *physical, inventory-heavy, manufactured* product with a real supply chain and a hardware-style compliance surface — a different machine from a coaching business or a restaurant group. It exercises: co-manufacturing/MOQ, inventory + cash-conversion cycle, DTC subscription retention, wholesale, and packaged-goods regulatory labeling. Figures are illustrative placeholders.

## One-line thesis

Emberline is a **premium, refillable home-fragrance system** — you buy a heavy, design-object candle vessel once, then reorder wax and diffuser refills on subscription. It turns a one-off, low-loyalty category (candles) into a recurring-revenue engine, sold DTC and wholesale in the US.

## The wedge (the one differentiated thing)

**Refill-first, not disposable.** Competitors sell a new glass jar every time. Emberline sells the vessel once as a keepable object and monetizes the *consumable* (wax/diffuser refills) on a subscription cadence. This is the closed-loop retention playbook applied to a category that almost never has it: a permanent object → a fast, visible win (a beautiful lit object at home) → recurring refills. Reason-to-believe beyond scent: lower cost-per-burn over time, less waste, a signature scent the customer doesn't have to keep rediscovering.

## Product line (deliberately tight — guards complexity bias)

- **The Vessel** — refillable 9 oz ceramic candle vessel, sold once. ~3 colorways.
- **Wax Refills** — the core consumable. ~6 core scents. Sold single or on subscription (every 4–8 weeks).
- **Reed Diffuser + Diffuser Refill** — second consumable line, same refill logic.
- **Discovery Set** — 4 mini scent tins; the low-friction entry / self-liquidating trial offer.
- **Limited seasonal drops** — 2–3/year; the "newness" and list-building engine.

*Held back on purpose (future expansion, not launch): room sprays, hand soap, body/personal-care, travel tins. Flagged to counter overbuild.*

## Customer (ICP)

- **Primary (DTC):** design-conscious homeowners/renters, ~30–55, household income $120k+, who already buy premium home goods and reorder consumables. They buy the aesthetic + refill convenience.
- **Wholesale:** independent home/lifestyle boutiques, interior designers and home stagers, boutique hotels and short-term-rental operators (amenity + retail resale).
- **Jobs-to-be-done:** "make my home feel considered and mine"; "give me one signature scent I can just re-order"; "a beautiful object that isn't disposable."

## Business model — how it makes money

1. **DTC subscription refills** *(primary)* — recurring, high-margin, owns the customer. Shopify + subscription app.
2. **DTC one-off** — vessels, discovery sets, gifts; seasonal (Q4 gifting) spikes.
3. **Wholesale** — boutiques, designers, hotels; volume + brand visibility, lower margin, cash upfront (prepaid or net-30). MOQ per account.
4. **Limited drops** — margin, urgency, email/SMS list growth.

## Channels & GTM

- **DTC:** Shopify + subscription app; email/SMS as the retention engine; Pinterest + IG/Meta paid *after* economics prove out; creator/designer gifting (unpaid) first; affiliate.
- **Wholesale:** line sheet + Faire marketplace + direct outreach to boutiques, interior designers, and boutique hotels; trade shows later.
- **Events/markets:** local design markets and pop-ups for cheap trial, cash, and product feedback.
- **Positioning before promotion:** lead every pitch with the refill-system wedge, never with "scent."

## Geography

- **HQ + pour studio:** one US metro (placeholder: **Austin, TX** — maker scene, central shipping). DTC ships nationwide (US).
- **Fulfillment:** in-house pour + pack initially → 3PL as volume grows.
- **International deferred:** EU/UK need CLP hazard labeling and differ on fragrance rules; out of scope at launch.

## Operational surface

- **Supply chain:** wax (soy/coconut blend), fragrance oils (from fragrance houses — IFRA cert + SDS required), wicks (cotton/wood), ceramic vessels, recyclable refill packaging. Multiple suppliers, MOQs, lead times (ceramics 8–12 wks).
- **Manufacturing:** start as an in-house small-batch **pour studio** (low capex, high control) → migrate high-volume scents to a candle **co-manufacturer** while keeping limited drops in-house. Migration threshold is an explicit economics decision, not a default.
- **Fulfillment & shipping:** heavy + fragile (glass/ceramic) → breakage and dim-weight shipping cost are real margin drivers; protective packaging is a line item.
- **Inventory / cash cycle:** fragrance oils + vessels bought at MOQ ahead of demand ties up cash; **Q4-heavy seasonality** (gifting) creates an inventory-planning and cash-crunch risk. Core finance-ops stress test.
- **Quality/safety gate:** every scent × vessel × wick combo must pass **burn testing (ASTM F2417)** before sale — a non-optional product-dev gate. Lot tracking on fragrance oils; batch records.
- **Team:** founder + 1–2 pour/fulfillment ops + fractional finance/creative; SOP-run by design to avoid founder-dependence.

## Regulatory / compliance surface (hard dependencies)

- **No medical / therapeutic claims (GUARDRAIL).** Cannot claim candles "reduce stress," "improve sleep," "purify air," "aromatherapy benefits," or any health effect — such claims risk reclassification as an unapproved drug (FDA) and FTC action. Describe fragrance sensorially only.
- **Labeling:** Fair Packaging & Labeling Act (identity, net quantity, maker name/address); mandatory **candle safety warnings** (ASTM F2058 — "never leave a burning candle unattended," keep from children/pets, etc.).
- **IFRA compliance:** fragrance formulas must respect IFRA usage-level limits per material; keep IFRA certificates + SDS on file.
- **Hazard labeling & shipping:** diffuser refills are **flammable liquids** → CPSC/FHSA hazard labeling and **DOT/IATA hazmat (limited quantity)** shipping rules; carrier/air restrictions may apply. A reason to consider candles-first at launch.
- **CA Prop 65:** some fragrance components are listed → warnings or reformulation may be required.
- **FTC Green Guides:** "clean," "non-toxic," "natural," recyclable/refill claims must be substantiated — avoid unsupported eco/"clean" language.
- **Business:** LLC; **product liability insurance is non-negotiable** (fire risk); general liability; multi-state sales-tax nexus for DTC; trademark search + registration for "Emberline" (open item).

## Founder-fit self-check (against PROFILE)

- **Compounds vs. dilutes:** the refill subscription makes it a recurring engine, not a one-off product — it compounds. **But it is a new front vs. Executive Edge**, so for the OS this is a **REFINE / sequence-behind** candidate, not an auto-GO. Stated honestly.
- **Economics before build:** unit economics close at target CAC; discovery set self-liquidates; refills carry LTV; manufacturing starts asset-light.
- **Sell before build / $0 until proven:** validate with discovery sets, a small launch scent range, and wholesale outreach before paid media or any co-manufacturer commitment.
- **Founder-dependence guard:** SOP-run pour/fulfillment; founder reserved for approvals and key wholesale/relationship calls.
- **Failure-mode watchlist:** complexity bias (too many SKUs/scents); too many concurrent fronts (competes with Executive Edge for focus); standards-as-bottleneck (endless burn-testing / aesthetic polish delaying launch); future-state bias (building the co-manufacturer relationship before DTC demand is proven).
- **Voice/brand note for downstream copy:** banned by default here — *wellness, holistic, balance, mindful, detox, cleanse, journey*; and no health/aromatherapy claims. Describe scent, materials, and craft precisely; no hype.

## Key risks

- Q4 seasonality + inventory cash cycle.
- Heavy/fragile shipping breakage and cost erode DTC margin.
- Crowded, low-moat category — the refill system + taste/brand are the only durable wedge; execution must be sharp.
- Regulatory missteps (a stray health claim; hazmat on diffuser oils) are hard dependencies, not formalities.
- Fragrance reformulation risk if IFRA changes or a supplier discontinues an oil.

## Open decisions (for downstream builders)

- Confirm metro/geography (Austin vs. alternative).
- Vessel make/buy: domestic ceramicist (premium, high COGS, slow) vs. imported (scale, tariffs, lead time).
- Launch scent count (4 vs. 6 — guard overbuild).
- In-house pour vs. co-manufacturer sequencing threshold (units/month).
- Subscription cadence + discount depth.
- Candles-only at launch vs. include the diffuser line (hazmat shipping argues candles-first).
