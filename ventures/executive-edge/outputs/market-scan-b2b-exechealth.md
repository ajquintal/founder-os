> **DRAFT v0.1 — Tony-facing sales-enablement. Not for external send.** Web-researched Jul 2026; every figure is sourced inline (numbered → Sources list). No medical/outcome claims — ROI is framed on retention, performance, and key-person risk only. Items I could not verify are flagged **[PROVISIONAL]**.

**Offer under the microscope:** a company buys a 6-month program for 5–10 senior leaders at **$30–48k/6mo (~$9–12k/exec/yr)** on the Executive Edge OS platform. Positioning = the **Executive Performance OS** — the execution layer (daily protocol, adaptive scoring, human coach, labs as calibration). Not a lab test. Not a wellness perk. Not concierge medicine.

- **Longevity/performance is the 9%+ tailwind that makes "executive performance" boardroom-legible now** — turns "nice someday" into "yes, this quarter."

## 3. The wedge (the sentence the CEO repeats)

> **"Everyone else hands my leaders a test or a doctor's phone number. This is the only one that runs the 363 days between the labs — a daily protocol, a real human coach, and a score that tells me whether my top team actually executed — for about what one of them already spends on a concierge doctor alone."**

No competitor in the set delivers **daily execution + human accountability across the whole leadership team, priced per-head.** Labs give data; physicals give a snapshot; concierge gives access; wellness gives a perk. The gap all four leave open — *did the behavior actually happen, every day, for the team* — is the entire category.

---

## 4. Three objection-preempts (each with the fact that defuses it)

**Objection A — "Why not just a corporate wellness vendor?"**
Because those are a headcount perk, not a leadership system. **Generic programs sit at ~20% participation when there's no incentive attached (RAND)** [10], and Wellhub-tier access starts at ~$12/mo/employee [9] — a fundamentally different job (gym access) at a fundamentally different value. This is a per-leader execution engine with a human coach driving adherence, not a marketplace login.

**Objection B — "My execs can already afford their own concierge doctor."**
Exactly — and they pay **$2–10k+/head/yr, up to $18–25k at the premium tier, for it** [4]. That fee buys *access* (same-day appts, 24/7 text) and a periodic physical. It does not buy a daily protocol, an execution score, or drift detection — and the company has zero visibility into whether the leadership team is actually executing. We live in that gap, at or below what they already spend.

**Objection C — "Will my execs actually use it?"**
The reason generic programs fail is structural, not motivational: no accountability loop, hence the ~20% floor (RAND) [10]. The differentiator here is the **human coach + daily score + drift detection** — the same high-touch accountability model people already pay concierge premiums ($200–800/mo) specifically to get [4]. **[PROVISIONAL: no Executive Edge cohort adoption data exists yet — do not quote a usage % for EE; argue the mechanism, and offer a pilot/engagement guarantee if pressed.]**

## 5. ROI framing — justifying $9–12k/exec/yr

Anchor the price against **what one senior leader costs the business**, not against a wellness budget.

- **A senior leader's comp:** average EVP base ≈ **$309,250/yr** [11]. $9–12k/exec/yr is **~3–4% of one leader's base comp** — a rounding error on the person it's protecting.
- **Cost to lose one:** turnover runs ~21% of salary for typical roles, but **executive/senior-level turnover costs up to 213% of salary (Center for American Progress)** [12]. On a ~$309k leader that's **~$660k to replace once.** Gallup independently puts replacement at **0.5–2× salary**, and **~200% for a manager** [13][14], and pegs total U.S. voluntary-turnover cost at **$1T/yr** [13]. *(SHRM's often-cited 50–200% range aligns but wasn't verified to a primary SHRM page here — lean on CAP and Gallup above.)*
- **The math the CEO can't unsee:** a full 5–10 person cohort runs **$60–96k/yr**. Replacing **one** burned-out senior leader can cost **~$660k** [11][12]. If the program contributes to keeping even one leader intact and performing over its life, it returns **multiples of the entire cohort fee** — the annual per-head cost is **~1.5% of the cost of losing that person once.**

**Strongest single ROI stat to lead with:** **Executive-level turnover can cost up to 213% of the person's salary — ~$660k on a typical EVP — versus ~$10k/yr to run the system that protects them (Center for American Progress; Salary.com).** [11][12]

*(Adjacent, softer support if useful: Gallup finds employees who feel their employer cares about wellbeing are more engaged and likelier to stay; OSHA cites ~$4 return per $1 in mental-health support [14]. Treat these as directional, secondary — the turnover-cost anchor is the hard number.)*
