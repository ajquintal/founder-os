## 1. Stress-test — idea filter & kill check

```
BET:        Launch Emberline — a premium refillable home-fragrance system (buy a ceramic
            vessel once at $58, then reorder wax/diffuser refills on subscription) sold DTC
            and wholesale in the US — so that a low-loyalty candle category becomes a
            recurring-revenue engine, within ~12 months.

VERDICT:    REFINE

WHY:        The recurring-revenue thesis is real and the wedge is honest, but two things
            stop this from being a clean GO. First, it opens a concurrent front against the
            live primary bet (Executive Edge) and competes with it for founder attention and
            cash. Second, the model rests on two untested, load-bearing numbers — refill
            retention and delivered-to-door margin — and the headline "discovery set
            self-liquidates CAC" is arithmetically false as stated. Narrow it, prove those
            two numbers cheaply, and sequence it behind EE. Then it can graduate to GO.

MAKE-OR-BREAK ASSUMPTIONS:
  1. Refill subscription retention — subscribers stay ~5–8 active months (LTV $115–150/yr).
     This IS the model; without it you sell one pretty object at a loss. — confidence L/M —
     cheapest test: pre-sell a small "founding refill member" cohort to a warm list + 3–5
     pop-ups/markets; measure month-2 and month-3 re-order before any co-manufacturer or paid media.
  2. Delivered-to-door contribution margin survives freight/breakage — a heavy ceramic vessel
     and singly-shipped $24 refill carry dim-weight freight, protective packaging, breakage
     and 3PL pick-pack that the 60–72% headline GM ignores. — confidence M —
     cheapest test: get one real 3PL + freight + breakage quote and do one physical pack-out;
     recompute contribution per shipment (see §3).
  3. Front-end acquisition math closes — blended CAC $28–40 vs. a discovery set whose ~$10 GM
     cannot self-liquidate it; only discovery→vessel→refill conversion can. — confidence L —
     cheapest test: run organic + creator-seeded + pop-up funnel; measure true discovery→vessel
     conversion and blended CAC before spending a dollar on media.

FAILURE-MODE FLAGS:  new-front (FIRES — competes with Executive Edge) · architecture-before-
     revenue (RISK — do NOT stand up co-manufacturer / 3PL / full SKU range before refill
     retention is proven) · standards-as-bottleneck (RISK — burn-testing every scent×vessel×wick
     + aesthetic polish can delay launch; the brief flags this itself) · future-state bias (RISK —
     diffuser hazmat line + seasonal drops + co-man migration designed before core candle refill
     is proven) · founder-dependence (LOW — SOP-run pour/fulfillment addresses it).

ECONOMICS:  unproven — the model plausibly closes at target CAC/retention, but two inputs
     (refill retention months; delivered margin after freight/breakage) are untested, and the
     stated discovery-set self-liquidation is overstated. Not "doesn't close," not "proven."

GUARDRAILS:  clear, CONDITIONAL on hard dependencies — no medical/aromatherapy claims in ANY
     copy; product-liability insurance bound before first sale (fire risk); DOT/IATA hazmat on
     diffuser oils (argues candles-first); Prop 65 + FTC Green Guides substantiation for any
     "clean/refill/eco" language; "Emberline" trademark clearance before public launch. No LYV
     conflict. Drafts-only and no-spend respected — but note this venture uniquely cannot be
     validated at literal $0 (see §5).

FOCUS COST:  Founder attention and capital diverted from Executive Edge, the live ~$1M primary
     engine. Inventory (vessels/oils at MOQ, 8–12 wk lead) ties up cash through a Q4-heavy cycle —
     cash that cannot compound EE.

NEXT:        Pre-sell a founding cohort (vessel + committed refill subscription) to a warm list
     and 3–5 local design markets/pop-ups; instrument discovery→vessel conversion, true blended
```

## 2. Market scan — category, competitors, wedge

**Market read.** Global home fragrance is ~**$14.33B in 2026**, growing at a **~9.2% CAGR** to ~**$26.51B by 2033**; the **scented-candle segment is the fastest-growing at ~10.0% CAGR**, and sprays led with 33.5% share in 2025 (Grand View Research). The US is the largest single market — estimated **~$4–6B** of premium home fragrance `assumed` (clean US-only premium figure not freely available; see riskiest-unconfirmed). Momentum is high, but the category is **crowded and structurally low-moat**, and the refillable/sustainable angle is already a live, intensifying trend: **Diptyque launched its first refillable candle collection and is making its classic candle refillable (Sept 2026)**, and small refill-first brands (ReCandle Co., Everly, The One Co.) already exist. Refillability is therefore **not a novel feature to own — it is a contested one**; the defensible edge is execution, taste, price tier, and subscription convenience, not the mere existence of a refill.

**Competitive positioning.**

| Competitor | Price / model | What they do | Weakness we exploit |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Diptyque** | Candle ~$40 (190g) / ~$96 (large) `web-knowledge`; refill collection live, classic candle refillable Sept 2026 `sourced` | Ultra-luxury French house; design-object jars; "pieces that last," refill = wax "replaced in one effortless act" | No accessible-premium price tier; **no subscription/auto-replenish** — refill is a manual luxury ritual, not a convenience system |
| **Otherland** | Single 8oz candle **$20** `sourced`; diffuser refills via Otherland×Pura; **no candle refill, no subscription** `sourced` | Design-forward mid-premium DTC; strong art direction; seasonal drops | **Disposable vessel** (no candle refill); no recurring engine; the "refill" is a plug-in diffuser cartridge, not refilling the object you own |
| **Keap** | Subscription **$44.50–$49.50/candle** (sub), **$54.50** one-off, $10 off, **1–4 mo cadence** `sourced` | Natural-wax candle subscription; scent-of-the-month; multi-packs | **Full new jar every ship** — you pay for glass again and again; "surprise scent" churn; not a keepable design object + refill |
| **P.F. Candle Co.** | Candle ~$22–$40; reed diffuser ~$28; **no refill/sub** `web-knowledge` | High-volume DTC + heavy wholesale; earthy CA aesthetic | Disposable; no recurring refill; commoditized across marketplaces |
| **ReCandle Co. / Everly / The One Co.** | Refillable candle systems; niche/small `sourced (category exists)` | The direct refill-first analog — proves demand *and* that the wedge is contested | Sub-scale brand/design tier; room to out-execute on **taste, premium positioning, and a real subscription** |

**Pricing benchmark.** Observed DTC candle range: **~$20 (Otherland) → ~$40–55 (Keap sub / Diptyque one-off)**. Subscription cadence exists (Keap, 1–4 mo) but ships a full jar; luxury refills exist (Diptyque) but are manual, not subscribed. Emberline's **$58 vessel + $24 refill ($19.20 subbed)** sits at the top of accessible-premium. Where to sit: price the vessel as a design object at ~**$54–64** (defensible vs. a Diptyque/Keap one-off), and keep the refill **visibly below the cost of buying a new $40+ jar** so the cost-per-burn story is real. The stated price points are reasonable — the risk is refill *delivered* margin, not the sticker price (see §3).

**The wedge (one sentence).** A **keepable design-object vessel paired with an auto-replenishing refill subscription at an accessible-premium price** — occupying the white space between $20 disposable DTC (no refill, no sub), $40+ manual luxury refills (no cadence), and full-jar subscriptions (vessel waste every ship). The wedge is the *system* (object + convenience + honest cost-per-burn), not "refillable."

**Riskiest thing the scan could not confirm.** Whether design-conscious buyers will actually **subscribe and retain on refills** rather than buy the pretty vessel once and lapse. Refillable candles *and* candle subscriptions both already exist, yet neither has produced a dominant refill-subscription brand — which suggests retention/repeat, not demand for the concept, is the unproven crux. (Also unconfirmed: a clean US-only premium market size — the anchor figure is global GVR data.)

*Sources:* [Grand View Research — Home Fragrance Market](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/home-fragrance-market) · [Otherland shop](https://www.otherland.com/pages/shop) · [Keap candle subscription](https://keapcandles.com/products/keap-candle-subscription) · [Diptyque refillable products](https://www.diptyqueparis.com/en_us/c/refillable-products-diptyque.html) · [ReCandle Co. — Is a candle subscription worth it?](https://recandlecompany.com/blogs/refillable-candle-collective/is-a-candle-subscription-worth-it)

## 3. Offer architecture + unit economics (before build)

**Dream outcome (customer's words):** *"My home always smells like my signature scent, from a beautiful object I never throw away — and I never have to think about reordering."*

**Value ladder (DTC).** Physical home-goods has no true high-ticket backend; the ceiling is a whole-home multi-vessel set, and wholesale is a *parallel channel*, not a ladder rung (shown separately).

| Rung | Name | Price | Billing | Who it's for | Why it exists |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Entry / front-end | Discovery Set (4 minis) | $18 | one-time | first-timer / gifter / sampler | low-friction trial; scent-match de-risks the vessel |
| Core / anchor | The Vessel (9oz ceramic + first fill) | $58 | one-time | design-conscious buyer | the keepable object; acquisition anchor |
| Order bump | +extra refill or wick tool at checkout | +$18–24 | one-time | vessel buyer | lift first AOV; seed the refill habit |
| **Recurring (the engine)** | **Refill Subscription** | **$19.20 / refill, batched (see below)** | subscription | committed buyer | the durable, high-margin revenue |
| Cross-sell / expansion | Reed diffuser + diffuser-refill sub | $32 + $28 | one-time + sub | multi-room buyer | second consumable — **defer at launch (hazmat)** |
| "Backend" analog | Seasonal drops · gift sets · whole-home multi-vessel | $58–150+ | one-time / seasonal | superfans · gifters · Q4 | margin, urgency, list growth |
| **Wholesale (parallel channel)** | Keystone: vessel ~$29, refill ~$12 | net-30 / prepaid, MOQ | boutiques · designers · hotels | volume + shelf presence; cash upfront |

**Economics gate (illustrative — the physical-goods correction matters more than the sticker price).**

*Headline vs. delivered.* The brief's margins are **ex-freight**. On heavy/fragile goods you must subtract pick-pack ($3–6), protective packaging ($1–3), dim-weight shipping ($8–14 vessel / $5–9 refill), breakage allowance (2–5%), and payment fees (~3%). Rough **delivered contribution**:

- **Vessel first order ($58):** $58 − COGS $16 − pack $6 − ship $10 − fees $2 ≈ **$24 (~41%)**, not 72%.
- **Refill, shipped SINGLY ($19.20 sub):** $19.20 − COGS $9.70 − pack $3 − ship $6 − fees $0.60 ≈ **−$0.10**. **The recurring engine has ≈$0 contribution if refills ship one at a time.** This is the single most important finding and it is invisible at the "refill GM ~60%" headline.
- **Fix:** ship refills as a **quarterly 2–3 pack** (or add a shipping charge / free-ship threshold). A quarterly 3-pack: rev $57.60 − COGS $29.10 − pack $5 − ship $8 − fees $1.70 ≈ **$13.80/quarter (~$55/yr)** — now the engine carries margin.

*Affordable CPA.* First-order delivered contribution (~$24 vessel) **does not cover a $28–40 CAC** — you acquire at a slight loss and recover on refills. With batched refills contributing ~$40–55/yr, Year-1 contribution LTV ≈ **$65–80**, so CAC must stay **≤ ~$55–65** to recover inside 12 months; at $28–40 CAC, payback is roughly the first order + 1–2 refill quarters. Plausible, thin, and **entirely dependent on retention**.

*AOV.* First-order AOV ~$70 (vessel + refill, or set + vessel). The **~10× AOV heuristic does not apply** — this is a recurring/physical model, so optimize margin-per-customer + LTV, not a front-end stack multiple. (Skill correctly flags 10× as funnel-only.)

*Break-even (fixed).* Launch fixed cost — ceramic MOQ + oils + packaging + product-liability insurance + burn testing + Shopify/subscription app — illustratively **~$15–40k**. At ~$24–30 first-order contribution + retained refills, break-even ≈ **several hundred vessels + their retained refill streams**.

**Self-liquidation read (corrected).** "The discovery set self-liquidates CAC" is **false as stated**: $18 set → ~$10 GM ≪ $28–40 CAC. Front-end self-liquidation actually occurs at the **vessel order (~$24 contribution) plus the first refill**, and fully **only with retained refills**. The set self-liquidates *only via* discovery→vessel→sub conversion — the honest CPA at which the front end pays for itself is **~$24 (vessel contribution)**; anything above requires refill LTV.

**Implementation priority.** Sell the *recurring relationship* before building the machine: pre-sell "founding refill members" (vessel + committed sub) to a warm list + pop-ups, hand-pour a small batch, lock delivered margin with one real 3PL quote + pack-out — **do not** commission the co-manufacturer, diffuser line, or broad SKU range until refill retention and delivered margin are proven.

**The one number the whole model is most sensitive to:** **refill subscription retention (active months)** — it converts an acquire-at-a-loss front end into a profitable customer and is the entire LTV. Runner-up: **delivered contribution per refill shipment**, which is ~$0 unless refills are batched.

## 4. Opportunity sizing — 12-month range

**Tops-down (ceiling only).**
- **TAM:** global home fragrance ~**$14.33B (2026)**, candles fastest-growing (~10% CAGR) `sourced [GVR]`. US premium slice ~**$4–6B** `assumed`.
- **SAM:** US × accessible-premium tier (~25–35% of category `assumed`) × design-conscious, refill-receptive buyers reachable via DTC + boutique/designer/hotel wholesale → est. **low-hundreds of $M to ~$1B** `assumed`.
- **SOM (12 mo):** a new, founder-sequenced brand, $0 paid media until proven, one metro, tight SKU → a tiny fraction of SAM → **~$0.3–1.5M** `assumed`. *Ceiling, not a forecast.* Note: for a manufactured good the real 12-month cap is often **pour/fund capacity and inventory cash**, not market share — a constraint tops-down has no slot for.

**Bottoms-up (the plan — DTC + wholesale + events summed, since physical goods runs parallel motions).**

*DTC:* reachable first-time buyers Year 1 from **unpaid** channels (warm list + creator seeding + 5–10 pop-ups/markets + organic) ≈ **600–1,500** `assumed (L)`; first-order AOV ~$70 `sourced`; ~40–55% start a refill sub `assumed`; retention ~6–9 mo `assumed` → refill rev ~$60–110/subscriber.
*Wholesale:* ~150–400 accounts contacted (direct + Faire) `assumed` × 5–12% conversion → ~12–40 accounts; opening order ~$300–800 keystone × 1.5–2.5 orders/yr.
*Events:* incremental cash ~$5–20k (some buyers already counted in DTC).

| Scenario | DTC | Wholesale | Events | **Booked** | Collected in window* |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Conservative | ~$60k | ~$9k | ~$5k | **~$74k** | ~$70k |
| Base | ~$117k | ~$30k | ~$12k | **~$159k** | ~$150k |
| Stretch | ~$196k | ~$70k | ~$20k | **~$286k** | ~$270k |

*For physical DTC, **collected ≈ booked** (cash at purchase, minus wholesale net-30) — the deposits/multi-month "booked vs collected" gap the method emphasizes barely applies. The real cash issue is the **inverse**: cash is tied up in inventory (vessels/oils at MOQ, 8–12 wk lead) **before** any sale, and Q4 seasonality deepens the crunch — net cash is worse than revenue suggests even though collection is immediate.

**Reconciled range.** Anchored bottoms-up: **~$75k (conservative) → ~$160k (base) → ~$285k (stretch) booked** in 12 months. SOM (~$0.3–1.5M) sits far above bottoms-up, so the binding constraint is **reach + execution + pour/inventory capacity, not market size** — typical for a new physical brand, and fine.

**Load-bearing assumption.** **Reachable first-time buyer count from unpaid channels** (pops/creator/organic) — it is the base the refill multiplier sits on; flex it ±50% and the whole range moves most. **Cheapest test:** run 3–5 pop-ups/markets + creator seeding toward a target of ~300 first-time buyers and a founding-refill cohort in 60–90 days; measure buyers/event and refill-start rate before funding inventory scale or any paid media.

**Assumptions ledger.** TAM/CAGR/candle-growth `sourced [GVR]` · US premium slice `assumed` · SAM filters `assumed` · SOM `assumed` · AOV/price points `sourced [context/brief]` · buyer counts, conversion, retention, wholesale account math `assumed` · delivered-margin corrections `benchmarked` (freight/3PL norms).

## 5. Go / No-go — commit gate

*The stress-test returned **REFINE**; per the gate's rules a REFINE must be resolved before committing. It is resolved here by **narrowing scope** to a bounded, candles-only, one-metro validation sprint and gating THAT — not the full build.*

```
COMMIT:      Fund a bounded, time-boxed validation sprint for Emberline — candles-only, one
             metro, ≤4 scents — to prove refill retention and delivered margin before any
             co-manufacturer, 3PL, diffuser line, or paid media. NOT a commitment to the build.

DECISION:    CONDITIONAL GO  (capped — it taxes the live primary bet and uniquely requires
             real upfront spend; sequence behind Executive Edge)

BASIS:       stress-test REFINE (resolved via narrowing) · market real & growing (candles ~10%
             CAGR) with an HONEST, contested wedge (system = object + subscription, not
             "refillable") · economics UNPROVEN (closes only if refills ship batched and
             retention holds; discovery-set self-liquidation is overstated) · size ~$75k–$285k
             booked/12 mo, anchored bottoms-up, capped far below SOM.

CONDITIONS THAT MUST HOLD:
  1. Tony explicitly approves a HARD-CAPPED validation budget — this venture cannot be tested at
     literal $0 (irreducible inventory MOQ + insurance + burn testing precede any sale). Escalated
     because it breaks the "$0 until demand proven" default. — verified by written sign-off before spend.
  2. Product-liability insurance bound AND burn testing (ASTM F2417) passed BEFORE the first sale. — by day 30.
  3. No medical/aromatherapy claims in any copy; candle safety warnings (ASTM F2058) + FPLA
     labeling on every unit; defer the diffuser line (DOT/IATA hazmat). — verified at copy/label review.
  4. Refills validated to ship BATCHED (delivered contribution > ~$8/shipment) — single-refill
     shipping makes the recurring engine ≈$0. — verified by one real 3PL pack-out + freight quote by day 30.
  5. Sequenced behind Executive Edge — the sprint runs only within founder time that does not
     fragment the primary bet. — verified against EE weekly capacity.

RESOURCE ASK:  ~$8–20k hard-capped (ceramic MOQ + wax/oils + packaging + product-liability
     insurance + burn testing) · founder ~1–2 hrs/wk (approvals + a few wholesale/relationship
     calls only) · ~90-day calendar · displaces founder attention + cash from Executive Edge.

FIRST FUNDED MILESTONE:  Hand-poured small batch (~150–300 units, ≤4 scents); pre-sell "founding
     refill members" (vessel + committed sub) to a warm list + 3–5 pop-ups/markets; one real 3PL
     pack-out to lock delivered margin; drafts-only marketing (nothing published/sent without
     approval). Budget: within the capped ask. By: ~day 90 from approval.

KILL CRITERIA (dated, metric + threshold):
  - < ~150 first-time buyers OR < ~40% refill-sub start rate across the sprint by day 90 → stop/reset.
  - Delivered contribution ≤ ~$8 per refill shipment after real freight/breakage AND unfixable by
    batching/shipping fee by day 30 → kill the DTC single-refill model.
  - Any medical/aromatherapy claim required to sell, OR product-liability insurance unobtainable/
    uneconomic → hard stop (guardrail).
  - Blended CAC can't get under Year-1 contribution LTV on organic alone by day 90 → do NOT graduate to paid.

GUARDRAILS:  clear, CONDITIONAL — claims discipline (no medical/aromatherapy), insurance-before-
     sale, hazmat handling (candles-first), Prop 65 + FTC Green Guides substantiation, "Emberline"
     trademark clearance, and ALL spend requires Tony's explicit sign-off. No LYV conflict.

FOCUS COST:  Founder attention + capital pulled from Executive Edge (the live ~$1M engine);
     inventory ties up cash through Q4. This is precisely why the venture is sequenced BEHIND EE,
     not run in parallel. If EE capacity leaves no room now, the honest call is defer, not launch.
```

**Why CONDITIONAL GO, capped — not GO, not NO-GO.** The thesis compounds internally and the wedge is honest, so it is not a KILL. But it opens a front against the primary bet *and* is the rare Founder-OS case that cannot be validated at $0 — so it cannot be a clean GO. The gate funds the **cheapest honest test** (a small pre-sold batch + pop-ups), not the build, and converts the two load-bearing unknowns — refill retention and delivered margin — into dated, killable checks. If Tony's focus/capital can't absorb even the capped sprint right now, the correct portfolio move is to **sequence it behind Executive Edge and revisit**, not launch.
