## 1. Positioning (skill: `positioning` — April Dunford)

Physical DTC + wholesale has **two structurally different buyers**, so this section carries **two** positioning statements. The skill's contract assumes one segment; the DTC consumer buys convenience + aesthetic for themselves, while the trade buyer buys sell-through + keystone margin for resale. Both are below. Lead with the DTC statement; the wholesale statement is the trade sell.

### 1a. DTC positioning statement (primary)

```
CATEGORY:   Refillable home-fragrance system (the object-plus-refill category) —
            deliberately NOT "another premium candle."
FOR:        Design-conscious homeowners & renters ~30–55, HHI $120k+, who already buy
            premium home goods and reorder consumables. Spot them: they own considered
            objects, repurchase staples on autopilot, and dislike buying the same thing twice.
WHO:        Want their home to feel considered and theirs — one signature scent they can
            simply re-order — and are quietly tired of throwing out a $40 glass jar every time.
EMBERLINE IS THE refillable home-fragrance system THAT gives you one keepable ceramic
            object and delivers scent refills to it on a cadence you set.
UNLIKE:     disposable DTC candles (a new jar every time, no refill), manual luxury refills
            (a refill exists, but you re-buy and re-pour it by hand — no cadence), and
            full-jar subscriptions (you pay for the glass again on every shipment).
BECAUSE:    you buy the vessel once as a design object, then only the consumable recurs —
            an auto-replenishing refill at an accessible-premium price, below the cost of a new jar.
PROOF:      heavy ceramic vessel (owned, not discarded) · refill priced visibly under a
            comparable new candle · set-your-own cadence · hand-poured, burn-tested (ASTM
            F2417), IFRA-compliant materials · [pilot metric to be measured: founding-member
            month-2/3 refill reorder rate — per 01-concept, this is the make-or-break number].
TREND:      refillable home fragrance is now a live shift (a major house made its classic
            candle refillable, Sept 2026). The refill exists; the object-plus-cadence SYSTEM
            is the part still unowned. (Use as support — never lead with the trend over the value.)
```

### 1b. Wholesale positioning statement (parallel channel — the trade sell)

```
CATEGORY:   A refillable candle line for premium home/lifestyle retail — a shelf story, not a SKU.
FOR:        Independent home/lifestyle boutiques, interior designers & stagers, and boutique
            hotels / short-term-rental operators. Spot them: curated assortments, repeat
            consumable categories, customers who ask "what's that scent?"
WHO:        Need product that MOVES and comes BACK — differentiated on a crowded candle shelf,
            with margin and low return risk.
EMBERLINE IS THE candle line THAT sells the customer a keepable object once — then a reason
            to return for refills.
UNLIKE:     commodity candles that sell once and compete only on scent/price, or luxury jars
            that a boutique can't margin on.
BECAUSE:    keystone pricing (buy ~50% of MSRP) + a keepable vessel + a refill the shopper
            re-buys = repeat trade demand and a story staff can tell in one line.
PROOF:      keystone margin · MOQ per account · hand-poured, burn-tested, IFRA-compliant,
            FPLA-labeled · Faire + line sheet · reorder logic built into the product.
```

### Messaging pillars (every asset repeats these)

| # | Pillar | The claim (one line) | Proof / why believable | Objection it answers |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 ★ | **The object you keep** (the wedge) | You buy the vessel once. Only the scent recurs. | Heavy ceramic vessel, owned not discarded; refill priced under a new jar; a category that almost never works this way. | "Another candle I'll throw out?" |
| 2 | **One signature scent, on your schedule** | Set a cadence; stop rediscovering. | Subscription cadence 4–8 wks; batched shipping; not a surprise-scent-of-the-month. | "I don't want to think about reordering — or get a scent I didn't choose." |
| 3 | **Accessible-premium, honest cost-per-burn** | Design-object quality; refill costs visibly less than re-buying. | $58 vessel / $19.20 subbed refill vs. $40+ new jars; the cost-per-burn math is real. | "Is premium worth it?" |
| 4 | **Made to a standard** | Hand-poured, burn-tested, made from disclosed materials. | ASTM F2417 burn testing; IFRA-compliant fragrance; soy/coconut wax, cotton/wood wick; FPLA labeling + ASTM F2058 safety. | "Is it safe / well made?" (**No health claims** — safety + craft only.) |
| 5 (trade) | **Sells through and comes back** | A keepable object + a re-bought refill = repeat demand on your shelf. | Keystone margin; reorder logic; a one-line story for staff. | (Trade) "Will it move — and re-order?" |

**Lead pillar now:** #1, the object you keep — it *is* the wedge and the one thing no competitor ships as a system.

## 2. Brand direction + design tokens (skill: `brand-design`)

> **OS-fit note (carried into the appendix):** the skill's design-token layer is built to paste into a `starters/saas` Tailwind/shadcn **product UI**. Emberline has no SaaS app. Its DTC surface is a **Shopify theme** (theme-editor color settings + Liquid), which does **not** consume a shadcn config — so the "zero-rework paste" contract does not hold here. Tokens below are provided in the skill's required `H S% L%` format (useful for the storefront's custom sections, transactional email, and any headless build) but are **re-pointed at Shopify theme settings + a physical print/packaging spec**, and the color table adds the **Pantone/CMYK/material** columns a manufactured good needs and the skill omits.

### 2.1 Brand foundation

**Attributes (from the pillars):**

| Positioning pillar | Brand attribute | What it forces in the design |
|---|---|---|
| The object you keep | **Enduring** | Weight, matte ceramic, tactile; nothing that reads disposable or trend-chasing. |
| One signature scent, on schedule | **Considered** | Restraint; generous white space; one accent, used scarcely. |
| Accessible-premium / honest cost | **Warm-premium** | Warm neutrals over cold luxury; approachable, not gatekept. |
| Made to a standard | **Exacting** | Precise typography, clean labels, real material/spec callouts. |

**Personality.** Archetype = **Creator × Everyman** (the maker's craft, made ownable — not aloof luxury). Voice = Founder default (clinical, precise, premium) **plus a warmth delta**: sensory and tactile where the product is sensory, never hype, never health claims. Brand-as-a-person: *"a ceramicist with a chemist's discipline and a stylist's eye."* Do: describe material, scent, and craft precisely; lead with the object. Don't: use wellness/aromatherapy language; decorate with unmotivated gradients or soft-focus "mood."

**Target psychology.** The buyer is buying *"a home that looks like I chose it on purpose"* and *"one less disposable thing."* The quiet fear is **taste-insecurity** — *"I want my space to feel considered, not like a catalog."* Never age, never self-care-deficiency. Premium lands when the object signals the owner's taste.

**We are / we are not:**

| We are | We are not |
|---|---|
| A keepable object + a refill system | A disposable candle |
| Sensory and material | Therapeutic or "wellness" |
| Warm, accessible-premium | Cold, gatekept luxury |
| Precise about craft & safety | Vague about "clean" / "natural" (unsubstantiated) |
| A signature you re-order | A surprise scent-of-the-month |

**Do / don't (checkable):** Do lead with the vessel as an object · Do state materials and the burn-test fact plainly · Do keep the accent scarce · Do describe scent as notes (cedar, black fig, woodsmoke), not effects · Don't claim a health/mood benefit · Don't say "clean/non-toxic/natural" without substantiation · Don't use banned register (journey, wellness, holistic, mindful, detox) · Don't over-color a layout — one ember accent, ~10%.

### 2.2 Visual identity

**Logo direction (concept, original only).** A **set wordmark** — "EMBERLINE" in the display serif (Fraunces), tight-but-open tracking, all-caps or small-caps, one weight. It signals craft + permanence without a literal flame cliché. Optional lettermark "E" (an em-height serif E with a single ember-fill counter) for the vessel base stamp, favicon, and wax seal. Clear-space = cap-height on all sides; min size 16px favicon / 9mm on the vessel base. One-color char + reversed bone. **Direction only — originate; imitate no existing mark.** A clean wordmark is a legitimate v1; commission the lettermark stamp later.

**Color palette.** 60% Bone surface / 30% Char + neutrals / **10% Ember accent**. Screen contrast validated (relative-luminance, not eyeballed). Pantone/CMYK columns are **indicative — confirm against a physical draw-down / press proof and a ceramic glaze sample before production.**

Modular scale 1.25. No mono (a physical brand has no data/code surface — the skill's mono step is dropped as SaaS-shaped). Rules: sentence case headlines (wordmark excepted); body ≥16px web; measure 60–75ch; weights limited to Fraunces 400/600, Inter 400/500/600; line-height ~1.5 body / ~1.15 display.

**Imagery direction.** **Product + in-situ home styling is the hero** (not "data-as-hero" — that mode is dropped as a SaaS trope). Three modes: (1) **still-life** — the vessel as object, warm directional light, matte ceramic texture, honest shadow, warm-neutral grade; (2) **in-situ** — the vessel in a real, considered home (shelf, nightstand, entry), lived-in not staged-sterile; (3) **process** — hands, the pour studio, the refill act (the wedge, shown). Avoid: soft-focus "wellness" stock, literal flame close-ups as the only image, cold luxury minimalism, anything derivative of a known brand's art direction. Sourcing: original photography (commissioned or `canvas-design` for graphic pieces) or licensed (spend = approval).

## 3. GTM plan (skill: `gtm-marketing`)

**[DRAFT — nothing sends, publishes, or spends without Tony's approval. $0 paid media until the refill-retention + delivered-margin gates in 01-concept clear.]**

### 3.1 Motion

```
PRIMARY:   Owned-audience + creator-seeded DTC commerce (brand/visual-led, $0 paid until proven)
SECONDARY: Wholesale distribution (boutiques · designers · boutique hotels) — a PARALLEL channel, not a ladder rung
PHASE GATE: Prove refill-subscription retention (month-2/3 reorder ≥ ~40%) AND delivered contribution
           > ~$8 per BATCHED refill shipment — on organic/warm channels — before ANY paid media,
           co-manufacturer, 3PL, or diffuser line.
```

Rationale: low ACV, high-volume, impulse-to-considered consumer purchase with self-serve checkout → this is **not** sales-led and **not** product-led-growth (there is no free trial or in-product expansion loop; PLG is a SaaS motion that does not map here). The honest motion is **DTC commerce fed by owned audience + creator seeding + local trial**, with **wholesale as a genuine parallel motion** (keystone, MOQ, line sheet, Faire) that also seeds DTC awareness. Warm-and-free first; paid only after the two 01-concept numbers hold.
