# Milpa — GTM Package (Positioning · Brand · GTM · Content · Social)

**Scope note (read first).** Milpa sells one thing — a **remittance transfer** — for a **$2.99 (waivable) fixed fee plus a disclosed 1.0–1.5% FX spread**. No SaaS, no subscription, no MRR, no seats, no software SKU, no value ladder. The paying customer (the **sender**, US-based) is not the beneficiary (the **recipient**, in Mexico or Guatemala); the brand and every asset must speak to both. The critical path is **compliance and licensure**, so this GTM is a **pre-license design**: it can be built and staged now, but no acquisition runs, nothing publishes, and no money moves until licenses land and Compliance/Legal sign off. Everything below is **[DRAFT]**.

**Standing claims gate (guardrails, always on).** Milpa is **pre-license**. No asset may state or imply that Milpa is currently a licensed money transmitter, registered with FinCEN, or authorized to transmit money until that is true and Compliance confirms it. All financial claims (price, exchange rate, speed, "safe/protected," licensure, consumer rights) are **regulated claims → route to Compliance + Legal review before publish**. No FDIC/insurance implication (remittance balances are not deposit-insured). No absolute speed guarantee ("always instant") — Reg E requires an accurate, disclosed funds-availability date. Pricing is **disclosed per transfer and evolving — confirm live before any customer-facing use**. Drafts only; $0 spend without Tony's sign-off; secrets names-only; irreversible actions (license filings, banking, fund movement, publishing, spend) human-gated with a rollback path.

## Part 1 — Positioning (`/positioning`, Dunford lens)

**Method applied:** competitive alternatives → unique attributes → value + proof → target segment → category frame → pillars. Two adaptations are flagged inline and logged in Appendix A: (i) the buyer is **two-sided** (sender pays, recipient benefits); (ii) the category is a **fixed, searched utility label** — Milpa should *win the default category*, not invent a new one (the Dunford "choose a non-default frame" move is wrong for a commodity consumer utility whose discoverability depends on matching the term people already type).

### 1.1 Competitive alternatives (what a sender uses today)
- **Cash-counter incumbents** — Western Union, MoneyGram (retail agent + bolt-on app). All-in cost often ~4–6%+ on cash; opaque rate; English-first counter feel.
- **Digital leaders** — Remitly (immigrant-first mobile), Xoom/PayPal (near-zero promo on small MX sends), Wise (radical rate transparency, thin on cash pickup + rural reach).
- **Bilingual-native challenger** — Félix Pago (WhatsApp-native, Latino-first) — already occupies the "for-us" wedge.
- **The real defaults / do-nothing** — keep using the corner-store WU out of habit and trust; hand cash to a *viajero* (courier); ask a friend who has a US bank account to send it. **These, not the apps, are the true alternatives for the underbanked, habit-bound sender.**

### 1.2 Unique attributes → value → proof (chain each; cut any without both)
| Attribute (the wedge) | Value the sender cares about (Spanish-first) | Proof (mechanism/structural — see note) |
|---|---|---|
| **Exact guaranteed-payout quote before payment** | "Sé cuánto recibe mi familia, sin cargos escondidos." | The all-in quote screen shows amount paid · fee · exchange rate · **exact amount received** *before* pay — a Reg E pre-payment-disclosure duty turned into a visible promise |
| **Deep rural payout reach (SPEI to any MX bank + Banrural rural branches, GT)** | "Llega hasta mi pueblo, aunque sea rancho." | Named rails/endpoints: SPEI (any CLABE), Banco Azteca/Bancoppel/OXXO cash pickup; **Banrural** rural branch network — reach the transparency-led digital leaders serve thinly |
| **Spanish-first, bilingual human support, ITIN-friendly onboarding** | "En mi idioma, con mi ITIN, con alguien que me entiende." | Spanish-first UI (not a translation bolt-on); bilingual support hours; CIP accepts ITIN/passport, not SSN-only |
| **Honest, narrow, disclosed price** | "Una comisión pequeña y clara — no me cobran por debajo de la mesa." | $2.99 (waivable) + disclosed 1.0–1.5% spread; ~1.9% all-in vs opaque 4–6% cash incumbents; the rate is on the receipt |
| **Reliability + proactive delivery notification** | "Me avisan cuando llega — no me quedo con la duda." | Real-time status + WhatsApp/SMS "entregado" notification; saved recipients; Reg E error-resolution + 30-min cancellation rights |

**Proof note (a genuine friction, logged).** The method's rule — *"no proof → cut the attribute"* — collides with a pre-launch trust utility: Milpa's deepest wedge (**reliability + trust + retention**) is exactly what it *cannot* prove before it operates. It has **no customers, no track record, no ratings** at launch. So proof here is **mechanism + structure** (the quote screen exists; the rails are named; the consumer-protection rights are real), not testimonials or performance data. That is honest, but weaker than the method assumes, and it means the positioning is a *promise to be earned in-market*, not a proven claim — the pre-license waitlist/CAC test (concept §1) is what begins to substantiate it.

### 1.3 Target segment (who feels the value most)
First- and second-generation **Mexican and Guatemalan immigrants** in the 6 launch states (CA, TX, AZ, GA, NC, FL). Hourly-wage (construction, agriculture, food service, hospitality, care work). Banked or debit-card-holding, often **ITIN**-identified, price- **and** trust-sensitive, sending **1–4×/month** to family. **Spot them:** already remits via WU/Remitly; has parents/spouse/children in MX or GT (often rural); lives in Spanish on WhatsApp / Facebook / TikTok; asks "¿ya llegó?" after every send. **Two-sided note (logged):** the **recipient** (rural parent/spouse, cash-pickup or basic bank account) is the beneficiary and the emotional center of the job, but not the acquisition target — the method's single "FOR" slot models one buyer; Milpa's job-to-be-done spans two people in two countries.

### 1.4 Category frame (win the default, don't invent one)
**Frame:** *"la forma honesta de enviar dinero a casa"* — the honest way to send money home. **Deliberately the existing category** ("enviar dinero a México / Guatemala," "remesas"), not a novel one: discoverability (ASO, search, word-of-mouth) depends on *being* the term people already use. Milpa differentiates **inside** the category on transparency + rural reach + for-us trust — it does not try to escape it. *(This inverts the skill's default category-design move; logged.)*

## Part 2 — Brand direction + design tokens (`/brand-design`)

**Six layers applied.** Two structural adaptations are flagged and logged: (i) the skill's token/component hand-off targets **`starters/saas` (shadcn/Tailwind web)**, but Milpa's product is a **native iOS/Android app** — the web block below is real (it serves the **marketing site + responsive-web fallback**), but the mobile app needs its own token pipeline the skill does not provide; (ii) the method has **no scaffold for regulated-disclosure UI** (the Reg E quote/receipt/cancellation screens) or **bilingual localization**, which for a remittance app are the most important, highest-legal-stakes surfaces — added below.

### Layer 1 — Brand foundation
**Attributes (from the pillars):**
| Pillar | Attribute | What it forces in the design |
|---|---|---|
| Exact amount before paying | **Claro (transparent)** | The exact-received number is the visual hero; tabular figures; no fine-print traps; the rate is shown, not buried |
| Llega hasta casa + name | **Arraigado (rooted)** | Warm, earthy palette from the milpa (maize/green/earth); imagery of real families/home, never stock-corporate |
| En minutos / en regla | **Confiable (reliable, safe)** | Calm, unflashy, stable; status is always visible; security/rights communicated plainly, not with fear |
| For-us / dignity | **Digno (respectful)** | Speaks up to the customer, never down; Spanish is first-class, not a toggle; no condescension, no poverty tropes |

**Personality.** Archetype = **Everyman × Caregiver** (of-the-people, unpretentious; provision and family). **Brand-as-a-person:** *"tu primo que trabaja en el banco y no te miente"* — the cousin who works at the bank and doesn't lie to you: competent with money, warm, honest, from your community. **Voice delta (large, documented — logged):** the Founder default is *clinical/precise/premium, English, power-words like "protocol/optimize/leverage/architecture."* Milpa's customer voice is **Spanish-first, warm, plainspoken, family- and provision-centered** — the premium/clinical register and most of the power vocabulary are **off-register here** and effectively banned in customer-facing copy. Sparing, warm **emoji are native and acceptable**, and **real customer testimonials with genuine emotion are the proof that works** for this audience — both are deltas from the skill's "no emoji / no crying-testimonial" defaults. *Do:* lead with the exact number; write like a trusted person, not a bank; keep Spanish primary. *Don't:* hype, condescend, use fear, or default to the clinical-operator register.

**Target psychology.** The sender is buying **dignity + reliability + being respected** — *"proveo para mi familia, y no dejo que me vean la cara."* The fear is coded as **provision and respect**, not aging or status: "what if it doesn't arrive," "what if I'm being cheated," "what if I look foolish." Premium here means **trustworthy and honest**, not luxurious.

**We are / we are not.**
| We are | We are not |
|---|---|
| Honest about the rate | A junk-fee cash counter |
| Spanish-first, for-us | English-first with a Spanish toggle |
| Rooted, warm, of-the-community | Slick, cold, corporate-fintech |
| Reliable to the rural last mile | Only good for big-city bank deposits |
| A simple, honest transfer | A subscription, a wallet, or a crypto play |

## Part 3 — Go-to-market plan (`/gtm-marketing`)

**Adaptation (logged).** The skill's default posture — *"warm-outbound + owned-audience, founder-led, sell before you build, $0"* — does not fit: (i) pre-license money transmission is a **federal crime**, so there is no sell-before-build; (ii) there is **no warm/owned audience** and Tony's professional network is the wrong ICP; (iii) you do not warm-DM hourly-wage remittance senders one at a time. The economics-and-CAC-discipline half of the skill fits well; the motion/channel/lifecycle/attribution defaults leak B2B/SaaS and are re-based below.

### 3.1 Motion
```
PRIMARY:   Community-led (trust + referral compounding inside dense diaspora networks)
SECONDARY: Performance-paid (Meta/Google/ASO in Spanish) + a referral flywheel (give-$10/get-$10)
PHASE GATE (two-stage, licensing-gated):
  Pre-license  → prove sponsor-bank LOI + a bilingual waitlist/geo-ad CAC signal (legal; no transmission)
  Post-license → prove M1 repeat rate + blended CAC ≤ ~$31 on the ACH-funding/bank-payout (margin-safe)
                 mix in ONE state, Mexico corridor, before scaling paid spend or adding states
```
**Rationale.** Identity/trust category where word-of-mouth compounds and the audience congregates — the skill's own **community-led** row is the fit, not sales-led (no ACV, no warm B2B network) and not classic self-serve PLG (regulated; can't onboard pre-KYC). There is **no "ACV"** here — economics anchor on **per-transfer contribution ($3.93)** and **first-year contribution/sender (~$94)**. *(ACV is a B2B/subscription construct; flagged.)*

## Appendix A — Skill-fit friction log (GTM/Brand/Content-stage hardening)

Where the five GTM/Brand/Content skills assumed SaaS / subscription / B2B / owned-audience / funnel patterns that did not fit a strictly-transactional, licensed-MSB, Spanish-first, two-sided consumer utility. Skill · what broke · how adapted.

**/positioning**
1. **Single "FOR" buyer vs a two-sided transaction.** The output contract has one segment slot; Milpa's payer (US sender) ≠ beneficiary (MX/GT recipient), and the job-to-be-done spans both. Had to add a two-sided JTBD note the framework doesn't model.
2. **"No proof → cut the attribute" collides with a pre-launch trust utility.** Milpa's deepest wedge (reliability/trust/retention) is unprovable before it operates — no customers, no track record. Proof had to be re-based on **mechanism + structure** (the quote screen, named rails, statutory rights), which is weaker than the method assumes; the positioning is a promise to be earned, not a proven claim.
3. **"Choose a deliberately non-default category frame" (Dunford category design) is wrong for a commodity consumer utility.** Discoverability (ASO, search, word-of-mouth) depends on *matching* the term people already type ("enviar dinero a México"). Milpa must **win the default category**, not invent one — inverting the skill's signature move.

The venture "delta" here effectively **overrides the entire default register** — a change the skill treats as a small tune, not a full inversion.

**/brand-design**
