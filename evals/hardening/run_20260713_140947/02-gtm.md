# Lustre — GTM Package

> Reading order: (1) Positioning, (2) Brand + design direction, (3) GTM plan, (4) Content system, (5) Social plan. Each section follows its Founder-OS skill's output contract, adapted to a **two-sided local-services marketplace** (supply detailers ↔ demand car owners; revenue = take-rate on third-party GMV, not product margin and not a SaaS seat). A **Skill-fit appendix** records where the OS leaked SaaS / subscription / B2B-sales assumptions and how this package corrected them.
>
> **Inputs pulled, not re-derived:** wedge, competitor set, offer/economics, sizing, kill-gates, and the go/no-go come from `01-concept.md`. This package composes them; it does not re-run `/market-scan` or `/offer-architect`. The Concept-stage siblings (`/stress-test`, `/market-scan`, `/offer-architect`, `/opportunity-size`, `/go-no-go`) already produced their outputs in 01; the Launch siblings this GTM would normally compose (`/landing-funnel`, `/direct-response-copy`, `/paid-ads`, `/seo-geo`, `/launch-plan`, `/metrics-dashboard`) are cited and routed here, not re-authored.
>
> **The structural fact that bends every skill below:** this is not one funnel. It is **two acquisition motions** — seed SUPPLY (vetted detailers), then generate DEMAND (car owners) — that only produce revenue when they meet as **liquidity in a zip code**. Every GTM skill in the OS is written for a one-sided digital funnel with a single buyer. Each section states where that broke and what replaced it.
>
> **Claims discipline in force on every line of copy below (guardrail hooks from 00/01):** (a) **FTC Green Guides** — no "eco," "green," "waterless," "non-toxic" claims; the low-water/water-reclamation method is stated **factually as an operating standard that meets stormwater rules**, never as a green benefit. (b) "**Vetted / background-checked / insured / guaranteed**" must each be **literally, currently true** for every detailer before the word ships (FCRA-compliant checks; verified commercial-auto + garage-keepers coverage kept current; a funded guarantee reserve). (c) **No unsubstantiated performance claims** — ceramic-coating durability only to the product's warranted spec (the no-medical-claims discipline, applied to a physical service). (d) **ROSCA / AZ auto-renewal ("click-to-cancel")** on every Maintenance-Plan CTA — clear disclosure, easy cancel. (e) **Worker-classification (1099):** copy must NOT imply detailers are employees or that Lustre controls their work — they are independent (set availability, own kit, decline freely, multi-home). This is a hard constraint on both supply-recruiting copy and consumer copy, not a formality.

## 1. Positioning (skill: `positioning` — April Dunford)

A two-sided marketplace has **two structurally different "buyers"** the skill's single-segment contract can't hold: the **demand** side (a car owner buying trust + time) and the **supply** side (a detailer "buying" booked, pre-paid work). They need opposite messages on different channels, so this section carries **two positioning statements**. Lead with demand (it is customer-facing); the supply statement is the recruit — and it is bounded by the worker-classification constraint (sell independence, never control).

### 1a. Demand positioning statement (primary — car owners)

```
CATEGORY:   Vetted mobile car care — a trust-and-guarantee layer over at-home detailing.
            Deliberately NOT "another on-demand car-wash app" and NOT a lead-gen directory.
FOR:        Time-poor, affluent East Valley vehicle owners (Scottsdale, Paradise Valley,
            Chandler, Gilbert, Tempe), ~30–60, HHI $120k+, single-family driveway, 2+ vehicles
            kept 3+ years. Spot them: they protect the asset, guard their Saturday, and will not
            hand keys to an unvetted stranger from Craigslist.
WHO:        Want a showroom-clean car without giving up a morning — or trusting a stranger with a
            $45k vehicle in their own driveway.
LUSTRE IS THE vetted mobile car-care service THAT sends a background-checked, insured detailer to
            your driveway — booked and paid in-app, backed by a damage guarantee.
UNLIKE:     Craigslist / Facebook mobile detailers (no vetting, no insurance, cash-only, may not
            show), fixed-location detail shops (you drive there and give up the car), or lead-gen
            directories like Thumbtack / Yelp (a list of strangers — no vetting, no guarantee).
BECAUSE:    every detailer is identity- and insurance-verified and background-checked; a guaranteed
            arrival window; before/after photos logged on every job; and a damage guarantee that
            actually pays out — none of which any incumbent in this metro ships.
PROOF:      FCRA-compliant background checks + verified commercial-auto / garage-keepers coverage on
            every detailer; logged before/after photos per job; in-app booking + payment held until
            the job is verified; a funded guarantee reserve. [Pilot metrics to measure in the
            concierge test: time-to-match, on-time-arrival rate, damage-claim rate.]
TREND:      the category's best-funded consumer entrant (Spiffy) VACATED the come-to-your-driveway
            consumer lane for B2B fleets — the affluent-trust lane is open. (Support only; the
            value leads, never the trend.)
```

### 1b. Supply positioning statement (parallel — the detailer recruit)

```
CATEGORY:   A booked-and-paid demand channel for independent detailers.
            NOT an employer, NOT a lead-gen fee, NOT a boss.
FOR:        Independent detailers and 1–3-person crews in the East Valley already running a side or
            main gig, own their equipment, want a full week without hustling for leads. Spot them:
            active on Facebook / Craigslist / IG, cash-or-Venmo, no marketing budget, 1099
            micro-entrepreneurs.
WHO:        Want steady, pre-paid jobs and fast payout without marketing, scheduling, or chasing
            money — while staying their own boss.
LUSTRE IS THE demand channel THAT fills your week with vetted, pre-paid jobs and pays you fast —
            you keep your equipment, set your own availability, and can decline any job.
UNLIKE:     Craigslist / Thumbtack lead fees (you pay for unqualified leads and still chase the
            cash), or a detailing employer (a schedule and a boss).
BECAUSE:    Lustre owns booking, payment, and collections; you net ~78% with payout on verified
            completion (Stripe Connect). Your independence is real and preserved: your kit, your
            availability, decline freely, work other platforms.
PROOF:      78/22 split; payout on verified job completion; you set availability and use your own
            equipment; no exclusivity. [Worker-class guardrail: independence is load-bearing and
            genuine — it shapes the product; it is NOT a marketing line the platform then violates.]
```

> **Why not one statement.** The demand buyer's value is *trust + time*; the supply "buyer's" value is *utilization + getting paid*. A single positioning statement (the skill's contract) would blur a consumer trust promise with a labor-supply pitch — and the labor pitch is legally constrained (over-selling "we train you / we set your schedule / wear our uniform" raises 1099 reclassification risk). Two statements, deliberately.

### Messaging pillars (every asset repeats these)

| # | Pillar | The claim (one line) | Proof / why believable | Objection it answers |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 ★ | **Vetted & insured** (the wedge) | Every detailer is background-checked, identity- and insurance-verified. | FCRA-compliant checks + verified commercial-auto / garage-keepers coverage on every detailer. | "I'm handing my keys to a stranger." |
| 2 | **Backed by a guarantee** | If something goes wrong, we make it right. | Damage guarantee + funded reserve; before/after photos logged on every job; payment held until verified. | "What if they scratch or damage my car?" |
| 3 | **It comes to you, on time** | Booked to your driveway in a guaranteed window — no drop-off. | In-app booking + arrival window; at-home service; SMS "on the way." | "I don't have time / don't want to give up the car." |
| 4 | **Handled, on a schedule** | Set a cadence; your car stays clean without you thinking about it. | $159/mo Maintenance Plan; monthly or bi-weekly; click-to-cancel (ROSCA-compliant). | "I forget, or I don't want to rebook every time." |
| 5 (supply) | **Fill your week, stay your own boss** | Pre-paid jobs, fast payout, your independence intact. | 78/22 split; payout on verified completion; you set availability, keep your kit, decline freely. | (Detailer) "Is the 22% cut worth it vs. my own leads?" |

**Lead pillar now:** #1, **Vetted & insured** — it *is* the wedge and the one thing no incumbent in this metro ships. Pillar 4 (the Maintenance Plan) is the retention/LTV engine and leads all *repeat* and *win-back* messaging.

**Deliberately NOT a pillar:** any "eco / waterless / green" claim. The low-water method is real and a genuine operating standard, but Green Guides bars it as a marketing benefit without substantiation — so it appears only as a factual line ("low-water method; no discharge to storm drains"), never as a value pillar.

## 2. Brand direction + design tokens (skill: `brand-design`)

> **OS-fit note (carried to the appendix):** the skill's Layer-3 design-token deliverable is engineered to paste into a `starters/saas` Tailwind/shadcn **product UI**, and Layer 4 is a web-app component kit. Lustre has **no custom SaaS app** — its MVP is a concierge landing page + SMS + Stripe payment links (no app at all), and the eventual customer/detailer apps are assembled from **off-the-shelf / no-code + Stripe Connect + scheduling tools**, not a shadcn build (this is the exact overbuild trap 00/01 flag). So the "zero-rework paste into the starter" contract does **not** hold. Tokens are still provided in the skill's required `H S% L%` format — and here they are *more* useful than for a pure physical good, because there **is** a real booking UI surface — but they are **re-pointed** at the no-code booking stack, Stripe-hosted checkout branding, transactional SMS/email, the **Google Business Profile** brand, and a **service/physical** surface set the skill omits (detailer ID badge, door-hanger leave-behind, the before/after photo overlay template, opt-in yard signage). Worker-class care is baked into the physical layer (branded detailer assets are **opt-in**, never mandated — a mandate is control, which raises reclassification risk).

### 2.1 Brand foundation

**Attributes (from the positioning pillars):**

| Positioning pillar | Brand attribute | What it forces in the design |
|---|---|---|
| Vetted & insured (the wedge) | **Trustworthy** | Credentials, badges, and the guarantee shown as facts and chips — nothing hand-wavy or decorative. |
| Backed by a guarantee | **Accountable** | Before/after proof is a first-class UI element; the guarantee is stated plainly, never buried. |
| It comes to you, on time | **Effortless** | Calm, low-friction layouts; a booking flow that reads as concierge, not a race-to-the-bottom app. |
| Handled on a schedule | **Composed** | Premium restraint; generous space; one scarce accent — the feel of a service that has it handled. |

**Personality.** Archetype = **Caregiver × Ruler** — the trusted guardian of a prized asset, with quiet premium authority. Voice = Founder default (calm authority, precision, data over adjectives) **plus a service-warmth delta**: reassuring and concierge-like, never salesy, never hype. Brand-as-a-person: *"a discreet estate manager for your driveway — exacting, insured, and never late."* Do: lead with trust and the visible result; state the vetting and guarantee as facts. Don't: hype ("the best detail of your life"), fake urgency, eco overclaim, or imply the detailers are Lustre employees.

**Target psychology.** The buyer is buying *"my time back, and peace of mind that my expensive asset is in safe hands,"* and the identity *"I take care of what I own."* The fear coded underneath is **risk, not vanity** — *"I don't want to get scammed, no-showed, or have my car damaged by someone I couldn't vet."* Premium lands here through *credibility and control*, not luxury signaling.

**We are / we are not:**

| We are | We are not |
|---|---|
| Vetted, insured, guaranteed | A Craigslist gamble |
| At your driveway, on time | A shop you drive to and wait at |
| A calm, handled service | A cheap on-demand wash in a price race |
| Precise about credentials & the guarantee | Vague about "eco / waterless" (unsubstantiated — Green Guides) |
| A network of independent pros | An employer / a gig-labor brand (worker-class care) |

**Do / don't (checkable):** Do state the vetting + guarantee as plain facts · Do make real, logged before/after the hero · Do keep the accent scarce (~10%) · Do describe the low-water method factually, never as "green" · Don't claim "eco / waterless / non-toxic" without substantiation · Don't imply detailers are employees or that Lustre controls them · Don't over-claim ceramic durability beyond the product warranty · Don't use the banned register (journey, wellness, hack, revolutionary, game-changer, guru, fake urgency).

### 2.2 Visual identity

**Logo direction (concept, original only).** A **set wordmark** — "LUSTRE" in the display face, confident and tightly tracked, evoking a polished clearcoat finish through *typographic restraint*, not a literal shine/sparkle/car-silhouette cliché. Optional **"L" lettermark** with a single swept highlight (a polish gesture) for the app icon, the detailer badge, and the before/after overlay watermark. Clear-space = cap-height on all sides; min size 16px favicon / 20px screen wordmark. One-color obsidian + reversed mist (knockout). **Direction only — originate; imitate no existing mark** (explicitly not the marks of Spiffy, Washos, MobileWash, or any auto brand). A clean wordmark is a legitimate v1; commission the lettermark later.

**Color palette.** Premium, trustworthy, automotive-adjacent without garishness: a deep obsidian ink (a black car's clearcoat), a clean cool-light surface, a trust-carrying deep petrol as secondary, and a scarce brass "lustre" accent. **60% Mist surface / 30% Obsidian + neutrals / 10% Brass accent.** All ratios computed (relative-luminance), not eyeballed. No Pantone/CMYK column is load-bearing here (this is a service, not a manufactured good) — but the accent's on-color behavior IS load-bearing for the physical badge/signage, so it is noted.

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Ink / **Obsidian** | `#12171C` | `208 22% 9%` | 30% | Mist / white | 16.6:1 / 18.0:1 | Body text + primary action (light) |
| Surface / **Mist** | `#F4F6F7` | `200 15% 96%` | 60% | Obsidian | 16.6:1 | Canvas / app / page background |

**Typography.** Both faces **open-license (SIL OFL) — $0, no spend:** **Space Grotesk** (display / wordmark / headlines — precise, modern, faintly technical; carries "meticulous premium service") + **Inter** (body, UI, labels, booking flow). **Mono kept — and it earns its place here** (unlike a pure physical-goods brand, where the skill's mono step is dead weight): **JetBrains Mono** for the *verification/trust data cues* this venture genuinely has — job IDs, before/after timestamps, "insurance verified" chips, payout amounts, arrival-window countdowns. Modular scale 1.25. Rules: sentence case headlines (wordmark excepted); body ≥16px web; measure 60–75ch; weights limited to Space Grotesk 500/600, Inter 400/500/600; line-height ~1.5 body / ~1.15 display.

**Imagery direction.** **Real, logged before/after is the hero** (it is literally the proof, the product, and the dispute record — one asset, three jobs). Three modes: (1) **before/after split** — the transformation, on a premium car, honest light (the wedge, shown); (2) **service-in-context** — a real detailer working a real driveway, human and present (trust made visible), shot to read *competent and calm*, not staged-sterile; (3) **credential / verification cue** — the badge, the "verified" chip, the arrival-window SMS, used sparingly. Avoid: the "car wash" cliché (suds spray, bikini-wash tropes), fake sparkle/lens-flare, cold stock, and — importantly — **do not stage a hose flooding the driveway** (it contradicts the low-water/no-storm-drain standard and would undercut the compliance posture). Sourcing: original (photos are already captured on every job for the guarantee — dual-purpose) or licensed (spend = approval); real detailers appear only with a signed release.

### 3.1 Motion

> **OS-fit note:** the skill's motion matrix is `{sales-led, product-led (PLG), community-led, channel/partner-led}`. **None** cleanly fits a two-sided LOCAL marketplace. PLG assumes a free-trial/self-serve *software* product with in-product expansion — there is no such loop here. "Warm-outbound + owned-audience first" (the profile default) is a B2B-sales posture that describes neither seeding a *supply* side nor acquiring *local consumers*. The honest motion is a **marketplace cold-start**, which the skill has no row for. Named explicitly:
