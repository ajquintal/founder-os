# Lustre — Venture Idea (pressure-test)

> **Why this venture exists (for the OS):** to prove the Founder OS works on a **two-sided local-services marketplace** — a machine whose money is **take-rate on third-party GMV**, not product margin (Emberline) or a service Tony personally delivers (Executive Edge) or covers/seats (Ethos). It exercises the marketplace-specific spine the OS has never been run against: **two distinct acquisition motions (supply and demand), cold-start liquidity, take-rate/payout economics, trust & safety, disintermination defense, 1099 worker-classification risk, and split-payments/payout rails.** It also forces the "don't overbuild the software" guard directly — the platform is a thin coordination layer, not the business. Figures are illustrative placeholders.

## One-line thesis

Lustre is a **mobile car-detailing marketplace**: a car owner in metro Phoenix books a vetted, insured detailer who comes to their driveway; Lustre owns the booking, payment, guarantee, and quality layer and **keeps a commission on every job**. It turns a fragmented, cash-and-Craigslist trade — no-shows, no vetting, no recourse — into a reliable, guaranteed, on-demand service, and gives independent detailers steady demand without having to market or chase payment.

## The wedge (the one differentiated thing)

**Trust and reliability in a low-trust trade.** Today you find a mobile detailer on Facebook, Craigslist, or a lawn sign: no background check, no proof of insurance, cash only, and a real chance they don't show. You're handing a stranger the keys to a $45k vehicle in your driveway. Lustre's wedge is a **vetted, background-checked, insured network + a damage guarantee + a guaranteed arrival window**, all booked and paid in-app. Reason-to-believe: every detailer is identity-verified and insurance-verified; before/after photos are logged on every job; if something goes wrong, Lustre's guarantee covers it. Detailers get steady, pre-paid demand and instant payout with zero marketing burden; customers get a service they can trust with an expensive asset. Against fixed-location detail shops, the second wedge is simply **it comes to you.**

## Service line (deliberately tight — guards complexity bias)

- **Express Wash & Vacuum** — ~45 min, low price, the frequency/trial driver. Low-water exterior + interior vacuum and wipe-down.
- **Full Detail** — the core job (~2.5–3 hrs, interior + exterior deep clean). The AOV and margin workhorse.
- **Ceramic Coating / Paint Correction** — high-ticket, scheduled, skilled-detailer-only; higher liability, higher take.
- **Maintenance Plan** — subscription (monthly or bi-weekly express/detail). The recurring-revenue, retention, and detailer-utilization engine.
- **Fleet / B2B** — used-car dealership recon, small fleets (contractors, realtors), property managers. Volume, contracted, fills off-peak capacity.

*Held back on purpose (future expansion, not launch): headlight restoration, engine-bay cleaning, RV/boat/marine, mobile tire & wheel service. Flagged to counter overbuild.*

## Customer (ICP)

- **Demand — primary:** time-poor, affluent suburban vehicle owners in the East Valley (Scottsdale, Paradise Valley, Chandler, Gilbert, Tempe), ~30–60, HHI $120k+, single-family home with a driveway, 2+ vehicles kept 3+ years. They buy back their Saturday and protect the asset.
- **Demand — secondary (B2B):** used-car dealerships needing reconditioning, small local fleets, luxury/relocation realtors, HOA/property managers.
- **Supply:** independent detailers and 1–3-person detailing crews already running a side or main gig, who want booked, pre-paid work without doing their own marketing, scheduling, or collections. Typically 1099 micro-entrepreneurs with their own equipment.
- **Jobs-to-be-done:** (customer) "make my car showroom-clean without giving up my morning or trusting a stranger"; (detailer) "keep my week full and get paid on time without hustling for leads."

## Business model — how it makes money

1. **Take-rate / commission on every booking** *(primary)* — Lustre keeps **~22%** of the job price; the detailer nets **~78%**. Payment is collected in-app at booking; the detailer is paid out after the job is verified complete, net of commission. **This is take-rate on GMV, not product margin and not a SaaS seat.**
2. **Maintenance subscriptions** — recurring GMV, higher retention, and steadier detailer utilization (the liquidity flywheel's ballast).
3. **B2B / fleet contracts** — volume GMV at a thinner take-rate but stable, off-peak, and less price-sensitive.
4. **Ancillary (deferred, low priority):** priority-placement fees for top-rated detailers; wholesale Lustre-standard supplies to the network. *Explicitly deferred to avoid overbuild.*

**Lustre's contribution per job** = take-rate − payment processing − guarantee/insurance reserve − support/ops allocation − blended CAC. The single number that governs the business is **liquidity** (matched supply ↔ demand in a zip code), not any one job's margin.

|---|---|---|---|---|
| Express Wash & Vacuum | $49 | ~$38 | ~$11 | Frequency / trial driver |
| Full Detail (sedan) | $189 | ~$147 | ~$42 | Core AOV workhorse |
| Full Detail (SUV/truck) | $229 | ~$179 | ~$50 | |
| Ceramic Coating | $699 | ~$545 | ~$154 | High-ticket, skilled, higher liability |
| Maintenance Plan (monthly Full Detail) | $159/mo | ~$124 | ~$35/mo | Recurring; retention + utilization engine |

- **Take-rate benchmark:** on-demand local-services marketplaces run ~15–30%. **22%** leaves the detailer enough to beat their own-marketing alternative — the anti-churn threshold on the supply side.
- **Lustre net per Full Detail:** ~$42 take − ~$5.80 processing (2.9% + $0.30 on $189) − ~$4 guarantee/insurance reserve − support/ops ≈ **~$28–31 contribution**.
- **CAC:** demand blended **$25–45** early (local SEO/Google Business Profile, geo-social, referral); supply CAC lower, but **supply is the constraint** — liquidity, not demand, is the gate. Paid media stays at **$0 until liquidity and unit economics are proven** (per playbook).
- **Payback:** a one-off customer needs ~2 Full Details to clear demand CAC; a **Maintenance Plan subscriber pays back inside the first 1–2 months** and is the real LTV engine (target 6–10 active months; ~$110–150 take/subscriber/yr).
- **Liquidity KPIs (the ones that matter):** time-to-match (booking → confirmed detailer), detailer utilization (paid jobs/week), and coverage (% of target zip codes with ≥2 active, available detailers).

## Channels & GTM

- **Demand:** local SEO ("mobile car detailing Scottsdale"), Google Business Profile / Local Services, geo-targeted Meta/IG, Nextdoor and HOA/neighborhood partnerships, referral/loyalty, realtor and dealer referrals. Paid media *after* economics prove out.
- **Supply (cold-start first):** recruit existing independent detailers from Facebook/Instagram/Craigslist, detail-supply stores, and car-wash job boards; referral bounties; "bring your own book" onboarding. **Seed ~15–25 vetted detailers across a few zip codes before any demand marketing**, so liquidity exists on day one.
- **Concierge before platform:** validate by manually matching real jobs (landing page + spreadsheet + Stripe payment links + SMS) in 2–3 Scottsdale zip codes — no app — before building anything.
- **Local seasonality (a real Phoenix demand lever):** year-round detailing weather; spring/fall peaks; **monsoon-season dust storms (haboobs) spike demand** as cars get coated in dust. Extreme-summer midday heat compresses viable booking windows and is a detailer working-condition constraint.

## Geography

- **Launch metro:** Phoenix–Scottsdale, AZ — specifically the affluent **East Valley** (Scottsdale, Paradise Valley, Chandler, Gilbert, Tempe): dense single-family driveways, car-dependent, year-round demand.
- **Expansion (deferred):** Tucson, Las Vegas, Dallas, other Sun Belt metros — the same playbook, **one metro at a time.** Multi-metro before one metro reaches liquidity is the overbuild trap; flagged.
- **Note:** any expansion **into California triggers the ABC test / AB5** and materially changes worker-classification risk — a strategic gate, not a rollout detail.

## Operational surface

- **Two-sided ops:** supply onboarding & vetting (identity + background check, insurance verification, skills trial job, equipment check) · demand support · dispatch/matching · scheduling & capacity · split payments + payouts · dispute/claims handling · two-sided ratings & quality control.
- **Platform (guard the overbuild):** a customer booking app and a detailer job app (jobs, routing, checklists, before/after photos, payout) — assembled **from off-the-shelf, no-code/low-code + Stripe Connect + scheduling tools first**, not a custom-built platform. This is exactly where the OS must resist the software-shaped default and where Tony's complexity bias is most dangerous.
- **Trust & safety:** background checks, insurance verification, a damage-claim + guarantee-reserve process, mandatory in-app before/after photo documentation to adjudicate disputes, two-sided ratings, incident escalation, and a deactivation policy.
- **Payments:** Stripe Connect for the marketplace split, detailer payouts, and 1099 issuance; funds held until the job is verified complete.
- **Water/equipment standard:** detailers use their own kit; **Lustre standard requires low-water / water-reclamation methods** so jobs meet stormwater rules by default — turning a regulatory constraint into an operating standard (and, carefully, a positioning point).
- **Quality:** standardized per-tier checklists, photo verification, periodic mystery-shops, rating thresholds, deactivation for repeat failures.
- **Team:** founder + a small ops/support core running SOPs (vetting, dispatch, claims); founder reserved for approvals and key supply/B2B relationships. SOP-run by design to avoid founder-dependence.

## Regulatory / compliance surface (hard dependencies)

- **Worker classification (1099 vs. employee) — the marquee risk.** Detailers are independent contractors; misclassification exposure exists under the FLSA and Arizona's common-law test. Genuine independence must be preserved — **detailers set their own availability, use their own equipment, can decline jobs, and can work other platforms.** Over-controlling them (dictating prices, mandating uniforms/training, exclusivity) raises reclassification risk. This **shapes product and ops decisions** (e.g., who sets the price) and is a hard dependency, not a formality. **CA expansion = ABC/AB5**, a different and stricter regime.
- **Environmental — Clean Water Act stormwater.** Wash-water runoff (soaps, oils, road grime) discharged to streets or storm drains is regulated; many municipalities prohibit non-stormwater discharge to the storm sewer. **Lustre's standard = water reclamation / low-water methods, no discharge to storm drains**, documented. Arizona water scarcity adds a low-water expectation.
- **Insurance (non-negotiable).** Platform general liability, **plus** each network detailer must carry commercial auto + general liability + **care, custody & control / garage-keepers coverage** (damage to the customer's vehicle while in the detailer's control). Lustre's damage guarantee sits on top of, and depends on, verified coverage kept current — not a marketing promise the platform can't back.
- **Payments & tax.** Stripe Connect; **1099-NEC / 1099-K issuance** to detailers; **Arizona Transaction Privilege Tax (TPT)** and **city privilege tax** (Phoenix, Scottsdale, and other East-Valley cities levy their own) — the TPT treatment of detailing/cleaning services must be confirmed with a CPA (a real, specific AZ wrinkle and a hard dependency).
- **Background checks — FCRA.** Running background checks on detailers triggers FCRA obligations (permissible purpose, disclosures, adverse-action notices). Use a compliant vendor; don't self-improvise.
- **Consumer / marketing (GUARDRAIL hooks).** **FTC Green Guides** — do not overclaim "eco," "green," "waterless," or "non-toxic" without substantiation. Truth-in-advertising on "guaranteed," "insured," and "vetted" — the platform must actually deliver each. Subscriptions must follow **ROSCA / state auto-renewal ("click-to-cancel") rules** — clear disclosure, easy cancellation.
- **No medical/health claims (GUARDRAIL).** Not human-health-adjacent, but the same discipline applies: claim only outcomes the network can actually deliver; no unsubstantiated performance or protection claims (e.g., ceramic-coating durability) beyond what the product warrants.
- **Data & privacy.** Customer home addresses, vehicle and access details, payment tokens, and detailer PII/background data are sensitive — protect them, never export or expose them, publish a privacy policy, and keep background data within its vendor's controls.
- **Entity & agreements.** LLC; platform Terms of Service + **independent-contractor detailer agreement** + customer terms; AZ business/TPT licenses; trademark search for "Lustre" (open item).

## Founder-fit self-check (against PROFILE)

- **Compounds vs. dilutes:** take-rate + subscriptions + genuine network effects (more detailers → better coverage → more customers → more detailers) make this a **compounding, recurring-GMV engine** — not a one-off. **But** it is a **new front vs. Executive Edge** and is ops- and liquidity-intensive to stand up, so for the OS this is a **REFINE / sequence-behind** candidate, not an auto-GO. Stated honestly.
- **Economics before build:** take-rate math closes at 22% with maintenance-plan retention — **but the real gate is liquidity**, not per-job margin. Prove demand density and detailer supply in a few zip codes before building or spending.
- **Sell before build / $0 until proven:** validate by **manually matching 10–20 real jobs** (concierge / Wizard-of-Oz — landing page, spreadsheet, Stripe payment links, SMS) in 2–3 Scottsdale zip codes before building a platform or buying ads.
- **Founder-dependence guard:** SOP-run vetting, dispatch, support, and claims; founder reserved for approvals and key supply/B2B relationships.
- **Failure-mode watchlist:** **complexity bias** (building a custom app / detailer SaaS before liquidity — the sharpest risk here; the "don't overbuild software" guard is central); **too many concurrent fronts** (metro expansion before one metro works; competes with Executive Edge for focus); **standards-as-bottleneck** (over-vetting so supply never reaches liquidity); **future-state bias** (designing multi-metro marketplace mechanics before proving one neighborhood).
- **Voice/brand note for downstream copy:** follow voice-and-brand.md — calm authority, data over adjectives, no hype. Describe the **service, the vetting, and the guarantee** concretely; **do not** overclaim eco/"waterless" benefits (FTC Green Guides). Avoid the default banned register (*journey, wellness, holistic, hack, revolutionary, game-changer, fake urgency*).

## Key risks

- **Two-sided cold-start / liquidity** — the classic marketplace killer; must be solved neighborhood by neighborhood, supply-first.
- **Disintermediation** — after Lustre introduces a great detailer to a customer, both are tempted to go direct (cash) and cut the platform out. Core marketplace risk. Defenses: guarantee + insurance + payout only on-platform, subscription lock-in, payment convenience, rebooking friction, ToS.
- **Worker misclassification** — reclassification liability if detailer independence isn't genuinely preserved; amplified on any CA expansion.
- **Trust incidents** — vehicle damage, theft from a vehicle, or property damage trigger guarantee payouts and reputational harm; the guarantee is a real, reserved cost, not a slogan.
- **Low moat / multi-homing** — detailers also list on Thumbtack/Yelp; customers can DIY or use a fixed shop. Trust, reliability, and subscription retention are the only durable wedge.
- **Take-rate pressure** — if detailers judge the 22% cut worse than their own leads, supply churns; the take-rate is a supply-retention threshold, not a free lever.
- **Weather** — monsoon dust helps demand; extreme summer heat compresses booking windows and is a detailer working-condition constraint.

## Open decisions (for downstream builders)

- Confirm zip-code focus (tight East-Valley cluster vs. broader metro).
- **Take-rate level (20% vs. 25%) and who sets the price** — Lustre-set pricing improves consistency but **increases worker-classification risk**; a real tradeoff, not a cosmetic one.
- Concierge/manual MVP vs. building an app — **manual first** (guard overbuild).
- Insurance model — platform master policy covering the network vs. requiring and verifying each detailer's own coverage.
- Contractor vs. employee model — contractor to start; note the CA-expansion reclassification gate.
- Subscription cadence, pricing, and disintermediation defenses.
- B2B/fleet at launch vs. consumer-only first (focus discipline).
- **AZ/city TPT sales-tax treatment of detailing — confirm with a CPA (hard dependency) before pricing is finalized.**
