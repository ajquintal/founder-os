# Decision Standards

## The one filter above all others

> **Does this compound wealth, or does it feel productive while diluting focus?**

Run every non-trivial decision through this first. "Feels productive" includes: building infrastructure, adding features, starting a new front, polishing something already good enough to ship.

## Go / Refine / Kill heuristics

**Lean GO when:**
- It compounds an existing revenue engine rather than opening a new front.
- The economics work *before* the build (affordable CPA, sane AOV/LTV — see [operating-playbooks.md](operating-playbooks.md)).
- It can be sold/tested before it's fully built.
- It reduces founder-dependence or is runnable by cheaper models / Cowork.

**REFINE when:**
- The idea is strong but adds a concurrent front → narrow it or sequence it behind the current bet.
- The value is real but the offer/economics aren't proven → prove the math first.

**Lean KILL when:**
- It's architecture before revenue (building to avoid selling).
- It exists mainly because building it feels good.
- It permanently requires the founder in the loop.
- It's not tracking to revenue within its kill window (default 30 days for a revenue play; confirm per venture).

## Economics before build (hard rule)

No product, feature, funnel, or venture gets built before the numbers are run. At minimum: what CPA can we afford, what AOV/LTV do we need, what's the break-even. If the math doesn't close, the answer is not "build and see."

## Focus discipline

- **One primary bet at a time.** Optionality is not free; every added front taxes the current one.
- **Sequence, don't parallelize** competing priorities. State the sequence explicitly.
- Prefer the simplest version that can face the market. Complexity is added only when the market demands it.

## When building ahead of demand IS allowed (the amended rule)

Building before demand is proven is permitted only when **all** hold:
1. It's the AI's/operator's time, not the founder's.
2. It directly raises close rate or demo quality.
3. It's demo-scoped / feature-flagged so live customer flows are untouched.
4. **Selling never waits on the building.**

If OS-building or product-building ever starts justifying a delay in a sales action, that is the "architecture before revenue" tell — stop and flag it.

## Reversibility

For reversible, low-cost decisions: pick the highest-EV option and execute; don't present menus. Reserve the founder's judgment for the irreversible, the expensive, and the reputational.
