# Voice & Brand Defaults

> **Confirmed (Jul 12, 2026):** this is Tony's universal default voice across ventures. A venture may add small deltas in its own context, but it starts from here.

## Default register

Write like **a brilliant operator who respects the reader's time and intelligence.** Calm authority. Precision over performance. State facts; don't hype. The Executive Edge shorthand — *"a brilliant physician who also runs a hedge fund"* — captures the ceiling: expert, exact, composed, never salesy.

## Voice principles

- **Precise, not jargon-heavy.** Use technical language only when it adds credibility, never to hide behind. Explain the term if it earns its place.
- **Confident, not aggressive.** State what's true. No fear-mongering, no competitor-bashing, no ALL-CAPS urgency.
- **Direct, not blunt.** Short sentences. Active voice. Clear next action. No "in today's fast-paced world" openings, no filler.
- **Exclusive, not elitist.** Speak to a high standard without insulting anyone who isn't the target.
- **Data over adjectives.** Specific numbers and outcomes beat superlatives.

## Formatting defaults

- Sentence case for headlines (brand names excepted).
- No more than two emphasis moves per passage; prefer a strong sentence over bold-everything.
- Lead with the point. Cut the throat-clearing.

## Power vocabulary (use when it fits)

Protocol · Optimize · System · Operating system · Data-driven · Precision · Performance · Baseline · Threshold · Calibrate · Compound · Reclaim · Edge · Architecture · Leverage.

## Banned words / registers (avoid by default)

Journey · Wellness · Holistic · Balance · Mindful · Detox · Cleanse · "Hack" (noun) · Revolutionary · Game-changer · Guru · fake urgency · crying-testimonial emotionalism · generic hype.

*(A venture may extend this list in its own context — e.g., a category where a normally-banned word is unavoidable — but these are the defaults.)*

## What this does NOT cover

Visual identity (logo, palette, type), imagery direction, and per-venture positioning live in each venture's context and brand kit — not in this shared default. This file is the *voice engine*; the venture context tunes it.
