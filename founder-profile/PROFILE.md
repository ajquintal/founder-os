# Founder Profile — Core

> **How to use this file.** Any skill, subagent, or Cowork task in the Founder OS reads this file first, then reads the active `ventures/<slug>/venture-context.md`, then does its job. This file is industry-agnostic by design. Anything specific to one business belongs in that business's venture context, never here.

## Who I am

Serial founder-operator. I build and run premium, systems-driven businesses across multiple industries — I am not a single-vertical specialist. My edge is **cross-domain synthesis**: I see brand, offer, operations, sales, retention, finance, and product *simultaneously*, in categories where most operators understand one lane. I think in operating systems and reusable platforms, and I hold a high aesthetic and quality bar.

## My superpower (what to lean on)

I see the **whole machine** at once. When a problem is framed narrowly ("fix the ads"), my instinct — and the OS's job — is to check the whole system around it (offer → funnel → retention → economics), because the real leak is usually upstream of where it shows up. Use me, and this system, for whole-machine thinking, not single-lane tactics.

## My failure modes (what every asset must guard against)

These are documented and real. Every OS asset should actively check for them and flag when it sees them:

1. **Complexity bias / overbuild** — I build the cathedral before selling the screwdriver. I add layers because building *feels* like progress.
2. **Too many concurrent fronts** — I run multiple ventures/ideas at once and dilute focus.
3. **Standards-as-bottleneck** — my quality bar becomes the reason nothing ships.
4. **Future-state bias** — I design the end-state before proving the core.
5. **Founder-dependence** — I become the single point of failure across everything.

## The governing question (apply to every decision)

> **"Does this compound wealth, or does it feel productive while diluting focus?"**

If an action, feature, or asset can't clearly answer the first half, treat it as the second half until proven otherwise.

## My standards bar

- **Finished assets, not frameworks.** "Here's a framework" is failure; "here's the thing, ready to use/send" is the standard.
- **Economics before build.** The math must work before anything gets built. (See [operating-playbooks.md](operating-playbooks.md).)
- **Ship live over perfect-but-late.** Iterate from real feedback, not from a private blueprint.
- **Premium taste, precise language.** Clinical precision; no hype, no fluff, no filler openings. (See [voice-and-brand.md](voice-and-brand.md).)

## Current mandate (context, updates over time)

- **Income targets:** $1M by end of 2026 → $2M+/year floor from 2027.
- **My time is the scarcest input.** Reserve it for approvals and sales/relationship calls only (~2 hrs/week on execution). The OS exists so cheaper models and Cowork do everything else. Never assign me research, writing, or setup.
- **Live revenue engine:** Executive Edge OS (see its venture context). Most $-leverage work compounds that today.
