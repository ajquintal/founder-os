# Operating Playbooks

These are methods, stated venture-neutrally. A skill applies the relevant playbook to whatever venture context is active.

## 1. Offer architecture

Design offers on the **value equation**: maximize (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice). Every offer decision either raises the numerator or lowers the denominator. State the dream outcome in the customer's words, not the product's features.

## 2. Value ladder

Structure revenue as a ladder, not a single price:

```
Entry offer (low-friction) → Order bump → OTO1 → OTO2 → Recurring → High-ticket backend
```

- The entry offer should aim to self-liquidate acquisition cost.
- In paid-acquisition / funnel models, aim to lift AOV toward **~10× the core price** via the bump/OTO stack; recurring, experiential, and enterprise models optimize margin-per-customer and LTV instead — 10× is a funnel heuristic, not a universal law.
- Always know the next rung: what does a buyer of X get offered next, and why.

## 3. Economics first (the gate)

Before building anything, run the numbers:
- Affordable CPA (what can we pay to acquire, given AOV and margin).
- AOV target and how the stack lifts it (toward ~10× the core price in paid-acquisition / funnel models only; recurring/experiential/enterprise models optimize margin-per-customer + LTV instead).
- Break-even (units/orders to cover fixed + acquisition).
- Maximum/first-year LTV per customer.
If the math doesn't close, the offer/model changes — you do not "build and hope."

## 4. Go-to-market

- **Sell before you build.** Prove demand manually before spending on infrastructure or media.
- **$0 until demand is proven**, then scale spend against proven unit economics.
- Highest-EV channel first (usually warm/direct outreach and owned audience before paid).
- Positioning before promotion: name the wedge — the one differentiated thing no competitor ships — and lead every pitch with it.

## 5. Retention & ascension

Design the closed loop: acquire → deliver a fast, visible win → measure → ascend or retain. Recurring revenue and LTV are won at the "fast visible win," not at the sale.

## 6. Operations & SOPs

- Everything repeatable becomes a documented SOP that a cheaper model / Cowork / a junior operator can run.
- Weekly operating rhythm: what shipped, pipeline in dollars, what's blocked, the ≤3 things that need the founder (each ≤15 min).
- Compound every asset: lists, decks, sequences, funnels, and SOPs are built to be reused so each cycle is cheaper and faster.

## 7. Finance discipline

- Cash and collected dollars over projections and plans.
- Budget ceilings are hard; spend $0 on unvalidated ideas.
- Kill anything not tracking to revenue within its window and reallocate.

## 8. AI-native build conventions

When the OS produces or oversees software work, it follows these (generalized from the Executive Edge repo):
- **Definition of done** = shipped + merged + verified in production, not "written."
- **Drafts / proposals, not autonomous sends or destructive actions** — a human approves anything customer-facing or irreversible.
- **Change policy:** know the allowed vs. disallowed change types before acting; prefer surgical, reversible changes.
- **Secrets never appear in text, logs, or committed files.**
- **Tests/evals travel with the asset.** No capability ships without a way to check it.

## Expert lenses the OS reasons with

When a task benefits from a proven mental model, reason through the relevant lens below. These are thinking tools, not gospel — cut any that don't fit a given venture.

**Offers, funnels, positioning, economics (core):**
- **Alex Hormozi** — offer design, the value equation, grand-slam offers.
- **Russell Brunson** — funnels, hook/story/offer, the value ladder.
- **April Dunford** — positioning and category design.
- **Scale Mechanics / Patrick** — economics-first, front-end self-liquidation.

**Demand & product (why customers actually buy):**
- **Clayton Christensen — Jobs To Be Done** — the progress a customer "hires" the product to make. Pairs with Hormozi on the numerator of the value equation.

**Operations, systems & scaling:**
- **Verne Harnish — Scaling Up / Rockefeller Habits** — priorities, a metrics rhythm, meeting cadence.
- **Michael Gerber — E-Myth** — work *on* the business; systemize so it runs without the founder (directly counters the founder-dependence failure mode).

**Finance & unit economics:**
- **David Skok** — CAC/LTV, payback period, cohort economics for any acquisition-driven or recurring model.
- **Cash discipline** — collected cash over accrual optimism; watch the cash conversion cycle.

**Hiring & talent:**
- **Geoff Smart — Who / topgrading** — scorecard-based A-player hiring; hire to remove founder-dependence, not just to add headcount.

*Add or cut lenses as ventures demand. Keep the set small enough to stay sharp.*
